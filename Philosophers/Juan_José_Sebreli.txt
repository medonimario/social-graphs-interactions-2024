'''Juan José Pérez Sebreli'''<ref>[http://buscardatos.com/personas.php?nombre=Perez%20Sebreli%20Juan%20Jose&cuit=20-04346956-9 "Perez Sebreli Juan Jose"], ''BuscarDatos.com'', Retrieved 29 March 2017 {{in lang|es}}</ref> ({{IPA|es|seˈbɾeli|lang}}; 3 November 1930 – 1 November 2024) was an Argentine sociologist, essayist and philosopher. Throughout his intellectual work, he concentrated on the notions of reason, city and everyday life.<ref>[http://talkingheads.wordpress.com/tag/juan-jose-sebreli/ Sebreli, el tiempo de una vida"], ''Parlante Posmodernidad'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref>

==Background==
Inspired by [[Gay Power]] movement, he was co-founder of [[Frente de Liberación Homosexual]] ("Gay Liberation Front") along with [[Manuel Puig]] and [[Néstor Perlongher]], in the last years of the self-called [[Argentine Revolution]].<ref>[http://www.clarin.com/mundos_intimos/Frente-Liberacion-Homosexual_0_863913741.html El Frente de Liberación Homosexual"], ''Clarín.com'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref> The organization of the group was an adaptation of the [[democratic centralist]] partisan model.<ref>[http://www.elortiba.org/perlongher.html Néstor Perlongher"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100609073843/http://www.elortiba.org/perlongher.html |date=2010-06-09 }}, ''EL ORTIBA'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref><br>
In years that followed the last [[Proceso de Reorganización Nacional|coup d'état]] he directed study groups that were called "Universidad de las Sombras" ("University of Shadows").<ref>[http://www.elaleph.com/libro/Los-deseos-imaginarios-del-peronismo-de-Juan-Jose-Sebreli/85309/ Los deseos imaginarios del peronismo"], ''elaleph.com'', Retrieved 25 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref> As suggested by its name, it had a secret status, which was a consequence of the [[Dirty War]], that is, political persecution and [[forced disappearance]] carried out by the military government, both being crimes against humanity.<br>
Sebreli is mainly known because of his past collaboration with cultural magazines, such as [[Contorno]] and [[Sur (magazine)|Sur]]. He was a columnist for ''[[La Nación]]'' and ''[[Perfil]]'' newspapers and [[Clarín (Argentine newspaper)|Ñ magazine]]. He engaged in several television debates with [[Hugo Mujica]].

Sebreli was hospitalized with [[COVID-19]], on 9 August 2020, at the [[Hospital Italiano de Buenos Aires|Hospital Italiano in Buenos Aires]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/cultura/juan-jose-sebreli-internado-coronavirus-trabaja-su-nid2418533 |title=Juan José Sebreli, internado por coronavirus, trabaja en su nuevo libro |author=Daniel Gigena |date=12 August 2020 |publisher=La Nación |accessdate=12 August 2020 }}{{In lang|es}}</ref> He died in Buenos Aires on 1 November 2024, at the age of 93.<ref>[https://www.lavoz.com.ar/espectaculos/murio-el-escritor-y-sociologo-juan-jose-sebrelli-tenia-93-anos/ Tristeza. Murió el escritor y sociólogo Juan José Sebreli: tenía 93 años] {{in lang|es}}</ref>

==Work==

===Populism===
In his work, Sebreli was radically critical of [[populism]]. His arguments are characterized by a sharp focus on the Argentine heterogeneous experiences of [[Peronism]], a movement he describes as [[fascist]] at its core.<ref>[http://www.revistaenie.clarin.com/ideas/filosofia/Argentina-populistas_0_812318771.html “Juan José Sebreli: “En la Argentina son todos populistas””], ''Revista Ñ'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref> He criticizes the figure of [[Juan Domingo Perón]], depicting him as an [[authoritarian]] and [[charismatic]] leader, thus making use of [[Weberian]] categories of thought. Sebreli stated that the persistence of Peronism could be understood if one considers that, as a matter of fact, "there are sectors in the Argentine society that have a (pretended) lack of memory and are prone to self-deception". In one of his latest books ("''El malestar en la política''", 2012), he argued that [[Kirchnerism]] is a form of "latinoamerican neopopulism" and "plebiscitary caesarism", for it perfectly contrasts with a [[democratic republic]].<ref>[http://www.lanacion.com.ar/1585378-juan-jose-sebreli-estamos-en-un-fin-de-ciclo-pagando-el-fin-de-fiesta Juan José Sebreli: "Estamos en un fin de ciclo, pagando el fin de fiesta"], ''lanacion.com'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref><ref>[http://www.infobae.com/notas/713259-Juan-Jose-Sebreli-La-idea-de-ir-por-todo-es-tipicamente-totalitaria.html Juan José Sebreli: "La idea de ir por todo es típicamente totalitaria"], ''infobae'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref>

===Irrationalism===
Sebreli is quite known for his criticism of 'irrational' theoretical inquiries. His arguments were an effort to corrode the foundations of [[psychoanalysis]], and it is for that reason he labelled such discipline as [[pseudoscience]], following [[Karl Popper]], as it lacks a proper scientific method.<ref>[http://pinchilonfonseca.wordpress.com/2006/10/26/jj-sebreli-contra-todos/ JJ Sebreli contra todos | Yo nací para esto], ''Pinchilón Fonseca'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref> He asserted that [[Schopenhauer]] is "the true father of modern irrationalism", and that along with [[Dostoievski]] and [[Nietzsche]], he contributed to the transformation of [[philosophy]] in [[art]], the [[aesthetics|aestheticization]] of life and thought.<ref>[http://www.elcultural.es/version_papel/LETRAS/21343/El_olvido_de_la_razon/ El olvido de la razón], ''El Cultural.es'', Retrieved 24 July 2013 {{in lang|es}}</ref>

===Existentialism===
Sebreli was a member of the so-called "first Argentine [[existentialist]] group" along with [[Oscar Masotta]] and [[Carlos Correas]]. He introduced [[Jean-Paul Sartre]] into the academic milieux of his time. Molded by the [[Hegelian]] and [[Marxist]] Left, he introduced the political thought of [[Alexandre Kojève]] and that of [[Tran Duc Thao]].

==Politics==
In 2002 he supported [[Ricardo López Murphy]]'s candidacy to presidency, something he later referred to as "situational."<ref>[https://www.lanacion.com.ar/493409-juan-jose-sebreli-en-las-elecciones-gano-el-que-vuelvan-todos Juan José Sebreli: "En las elecciones ganó el que vuelvan todos"], ''La Nación'', Retrieved 01 March 2018 {{in lang|es}}</ref> Sebreli identified as [[social-democrat]], "in a European sense," and more recently as a [[left-liberal]].<ref>[https://www.lanacion.com.ar/2099847-juan-jose-sebreli-si-se-pierde-esta-oportunidad-vuelve-seguro-el-populismo-mas-acerrimo Juan José Sebreli: "Si se pierde esta oportunidad, vuelve seguro el populismo más acérrimo"], ''La Nación'', Retrieved 01 March 2018 {{in lang|es}}</ref>

In the wake of the abortion debate in Argentina, he addressed the subject expressing his [[pro-choice]] views at the Congress.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eCcrunMhx4I Juan José Sebreli sobre el aborto], ''YouTube'', Retrieved 24 January 2019 {{in lang|es}}</ref>

*1966: Eva Perón, aventurera ó militante ("[[Eva Perón]], adventurer or militant")

*1983: Los deseos imaginarios del peronismo ("The imaginary desires of Peronism")

*1991: El asedio a la modernidad ("The siege to modernity")
*1994: El vacilar de las cosas ("Hesitation of things")
*1997: Escritos sobre escritos, ciudades bajo ciudades ("Writings upon writings, cities below cities")
*1998: La era del fútbol ("[[Soccer]] [[era]]")
*2000: Las aventuras de la vanguardia ("The avant-garde adventures")
*2002: Crítica de las ideas políticas argentinas ("Critic of the Argentine political ideas")
*2005: El tiempo de una vida (autobiografía) ("The time of a life. Autobiography")
*2006: El olvido de la razón. Un recorrido crítico por la filosofía contemporánea ("The oblivion of reason. A critical tour through [[contemporary philosophy]]")
