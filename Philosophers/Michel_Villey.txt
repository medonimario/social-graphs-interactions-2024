'''Michel Villey''' (4 April 1914 – 24 July 1988) was a French [[legal philosopher]] and historian.<ref>{{cite book |last=MacCormick |first=Neil |author-link=Neil MacCormick |chapter=Villey, Michel (1914–88) |editor-first=Edward |editor-last=Craig |title=Routledge Encyclopedia of Philosophy |publisher=Taylor and Francis |year=1998 |doi=10.4324/9780415249126-T054-1|isbn=9780415250696 }}</ref> He was a professor at the [[University of Strasbourg]] and then at the [[University of Paris]]. He was born in [[Caen]] and was the grandson of philosopher [[Émile Boutroux]].<ref>{{cite book |first=André Jean |last=Arnaud |chapter=Michel Villey (1914-1988) |editor1-first=Enrico |editor1-last=Pattaro |editor2-first=Corrado |editor2-last=Roversi |title=A Treatise of Legal Philosophy and General Jurisprudence: Volume 12, Legal Philosophy in the Twentieth Century: The Civil Law World |year=2016 |isbn=9789400714793 |page=442}}</ref> The Institut Michel-Villey is named in his honor.

==Publications==

* ''Le droit et les droits de l'homme'' (PUF, Paris 1983).
