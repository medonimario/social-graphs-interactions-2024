'''Robert Desgabets''' ({{IPA|fr|deɡabɛ|lang}}; 1610–1678) was a French [[Cartesianism|Cartesian]] [[philosopher]] and [[Order of Saint Benedict|Benedictine]] [[Prior (ecclesiastical)|prior]], born in [[Ancemont]].  He published two book-length philosophical works in his lifetime, {{Lang|fr|Considérations sur l'état présent de la controverse touchant le T. S. Sacrement de l'autel}} (''Considerations on the present state of the controversy concerning the Eucharist'') in 1671 and {{Lang|fr|Critique de la critique de la recherche de la verité}} (''Critique of the critique of the search for truth'') in 1675.

In July 1658, Desgabets introduced the concept of [[blood transfusion]] (human-to-human) or [[xenotransfusion]] (animal-to-human) at a meeting of [[Henri Louis Habert de Montmor]]'s scientific society, which would later become the [[French Academy of Sciences]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite book |last=Easton |first=Patricia |url=https://www.researchgate.net/publication/44384194_The_Father_of_Cartesian_Empiricism_Robert_Desgabets_on_the_physics_and_metaphysics_of_blood_transfusion |title=Cartesian Empiricisms |publisher=Springer |year=2013 |isbn=978-94-007-7689-0 |editor-last=Dobre |editor-first=Mihnea |volume=31 |place=Dordrecht |chapter=Robert Desgabets on the Physics and Metaphysics of Blood Transfusion |doi=10.1007/978-94-007-7690-6_8 |access-date=2023-10-21 |editor2-last=Nyden |editor2-first=Tammy}}</ref><ref name="main">{{cite journal |last1=Roux |first1=Françoise A. |last2=Saï |first2=Pierre |last3=Deschamps |first3=Jack-Yves |date=2 May 2007 |title=Xenotransfusions, past and present |url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1399-3089.2007.00404.x |journal=Xenotransplantation |volume=14 |issue=3 |pages=208–216 |doi=10.1111/j.1399-3089.2007.00404.x |issn=0908-665X |pmid=17489860 |s2cid=42016125}}</ref> He would later publish {{Lang|fr|Discours de la communication ou transfusion du sang}} (''Discourse on the Communication or Transfusion of Blood'') in 1668.<ref name=":0" />
