{{Infobox person/Wikidata|fetchwikidata=ALL}}'''Laurens Perseus Hickok''' (December 20, 1798 &ndash; May 7, 1888), American [[philosopher]] and [[Anglicanism#Anglican divines|divine]], was born in Connecticut.

==Biography==
He took his degree at [[Union College]] in 1820. Until 1836 he was occupied in active pastoral work, and was then appointed professor of [[theology]] at the [[Western Reserve Academy|Western Reserve College]], Ohio, and later (1844â€“1852) at the [[Auburn Theological Seminary]] in [[Auburn, New York]].

From this post he was elected vice-president of Union College and professor of mental and moral science. In 1866, he succeeded Dr. E. Nott as president, but in July 1868 retired to [[Amherst, Massachusetts]], where he devoted himself to writing and study. A collected edition of his principal works was published at Boston in 1875. The Archives and Special Collections at [[Amherst College]] holds a collection of his papers.
