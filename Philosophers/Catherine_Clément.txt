'''Catherine Clément''' ({{IPA|fr|klemɑ̃|lang}}; born 10 February 1939) is a French [[philosopher]], [[novelist]], [[feminist]], and [[literary critic]], born in [[Boulogne-Billancourt]]. She received a degree in philosophy from the [[École Normale Supérieure]], and studied under its faculty [[Claude Lévi-Strauss]] and [[Jacques Lacan]], working in the fields of anthropology and psychoanalysis. A member of the school of [[French feminism]] and [[écriture féminine]], she has published books with [[Hélène Cixous]] and [[Julia Kristeva]]. She has also made contributions to [[musicology]]; her ''[[Opera, or the Undoing of Women]]'' (1979) is notable for its emphasis on the representation of women in opera.

She lived many years overseas, as her husband, [[:fr:André Lewin|André Lewin]] (1934–2012), was a diplomat who was France's Ambassador to India, Austria, Guinea, Gambia and Senegal.<ref>[http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-opinion/he-transformed-western-worlds-outlook-of-primitive-societies/article132734.ece The Hindu, Obituary of Claude Levi-Strauss, 5 November 2009]. Retrieved 20 May 2016</ref> She was awarded the Grand Officer of the [[Ordre national du Mérite]] in 2012<ref>{{cite web |title=Décret du 14 novembre 2012 portant élévation aux dignités de grand'croix et de grand officier |url=https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/jorf/id/JORFTEXT000026630693 |publisher=[[Légifrance]] |access-date=30 August 2022 |language=fr}}</ref> and the Commander of the [[Legion of Honour]] in 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Décret du 30 décembre 2017 portant promotion |url=https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/jorf/id/JORFTEXT000036339821 |publisher=[[Légifrance]] |access-date=30 August 2022 |language=fr}}</ref>
