'''Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez''' (September 17, 1915 – July 8, 2011)<ref>[http://www.jornada.unam.mx/ultimas/2011/07/08/13213758-fallece-el-filosofo-y-academico-adolfo-sanchez-vazquez/ Fallece el filósofo y escritor Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110710065709/http://www.jornada.unam.mx/ultimas/2011/07/08/13213758-fallece-el-filosofo-y-academico-adolfo-sanchez-vazquez/ |date=July 10, 2011 }} {{in lang|es}}</ref> was a [[Spain|Spanish-born]] [[Mexico|Mexican]] philosopher, writer and professor born in [[Algeciras]], [[Andalucia]].

==Biography==
After studying philosophy at the [[Complutense University of Madrid|University of Madrid]], Vázquez emigrated to Mexico in 1939 with thousands of other intellectuals, scientists and artists following the defeat of the Republic in the [[Spanish Civil War]], in which he participated as editor of the central publication of the Juventudes Socialistas Unificadas (JSU) “Ahora”.<ref>Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez would later write of this position: “Bearing in mind that we are talking about the central publication of the most important youth organisation in the Republican zone, with more than 200,000 members, and the enormous influence that it exerted through these members on the Popular Army, this was an enormous responsibility given my 21 years of age” (A. Sánchez Vázquez, “Vida y filosofía”, ''Anthropos'', Barcelona, no. 52, August 1985, p. 12.)</ref> Sánchez was appointed a full-time professor of philosophy at the [[National Autonomous University of Mexico]] in 1959, becoming a [[professor emeritus]] of the university in 1985.<ref>Galván Chávez, Ana. "Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez y el marxismo" ("Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez and Marxism") Retrieved 22 Mar. 2009. {{cite web|url=http://www.rolandocordera.org.mx/confi/galvan.htm |title=Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez y el marxismo |accessdate=2006-04-05 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060721213305/http://www.rolandocordera.org.mx/confi/galvan.htm |archivedate=2006-07-21 }}</ref> Sánchez also held honorary doctoral degrees from the [[Universidad Autónoma de Puebla]] and the [[University of Cádiz]] (Spain).<ref name="Ana Galván Chávez">Galván Chávez, Ana. "Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez y el marxismo" ("Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez and Marxism"). Retrieved 22 Mar. 2009. {{cite web|url=http://www.rolandocordera.org.mx/confi/galvan.htm |title=Adolfo Sánchez Vázquez y el marxismo |accessdate=2006-04-05 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20060721213305/http://www.rolandocordera.org.mx/confi/galvan.htm |archivedate=2006-07-21 }}</ref>

He embraced [[Marxism]], although an open, renovating, critical and non-dogmatic version of it.  His fresh interpretation of Marxism ran parallel to that of the [[Frankfurt School]]. In fact, ''The Philosophy of Praxis'' was published at around the same time as [[Herbert Marcuse]] was writing his '' [[One Dimensional Man]]''. In regard to [[ethics]], he opposed [[Normative ethics|normativism]].

==Philosophical works==

* ''Anthology. Texts of Aesthetics and Theory of Art'' (1972)
* ''Art and Society: Essays in Marxist Aesthetics'' (1973)
* ''From Scientific Socialism to Utopian Socialism'' (1975).
