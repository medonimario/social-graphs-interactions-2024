'''Agon Hamza''' (born 1984) is a philosopher and a political theorist from [[Kosovo]].<ref>Alfie Bown (2016). [https://hkrbooks.com/2016/07/11/hkrb-interviews-agon-hamza/ HKRB Interviews: Agon Hamza], Hong Kong Review of Books, July 2016</ref> Influenced by Žižek and his readings of [[German Idealism]], Marx and Marxist tradition in general; his work develops further the Hegelian-Marxist concepts of [[state (polity)|state]], [[religion]] and [[politics]]. He is the author of ''Althusser and Pasolini: Philosophy, Marxism and Film'' (2016). and the co-author of ''Reading Marx'' (2018) with [[Slavoj Žižek]] and [[Frank Ruda]] and ''From Myth to Symptom: The Case of Kosovo'' (2013) with [[Slavoj Žižek]].

He is the founder and co-editor-in-chief of the international journal of political thought and philosophy ''[http://www.crisiscritique.org/notes.html Crisis and Critique]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lareviewofbooks.org/contributor/agon-hamza/|title=Los Angeles Review of Books}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://crisiscritique.org/index.html|title=Home}}</ref>

==Life==
Hamza is an Assistant professor of political philosophy at Institute of Social Sciences and Humanities, in [[Skopje]], [[North Macedonia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.isshs.edu.mk/team-member/7141/|title = » Agon Hamza}}</ref> He briefly served as political advisor to the Prime Minister of the Republic of Kosovo, [[Albin Kurti]], in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://kryeministri.rks-gov.net/en/kryeministri-kurti-ka-emeruar-z-agon-hamza-keshilltar-politik-2/|title = Prime Minister Kurti has appointed Mr. Agon Hamza, political adviser|date = 10 March 2020}}</ref> He received his Ph.D. in 2017 from The Research Center of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts (ZRC SAZU), under the supervision of [[Jelica Šumič]] on the work of the French [[Marxist]] philosopher [[Louis Althusser]]. He is a regular contributor to a daily newspaper in Kosovo,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.koha.net/agon-hamza/129/|title=Autori}}</ref> and contributed from 2020 to 2021 to the '''Philosophical Salon''' in a monthly feature, "The R-Files” (short for “The Review Files”), with Frank Ruda.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thephilosophicalsalon.com/?s=R-files|title = You searched for R-files}}</ref> He has written for [[Al Jazeera Media Network|Al-Jazeera]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/author/agon-hamza|title = Agon Hamza &#124; al Jazeera News &#124; Today's latest from al Jazeera}}</ref> Los Angeles Review of Books (LARB).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lareviewofbooks.org/contributor/agon-hamza/|title=Los Angeles Review of Books}}</ref>
