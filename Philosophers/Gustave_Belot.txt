{{Short description|French philosopher and educational administrator}}
'''Gustave Belot''' (7 August 1859 – 21 December 1929) was a French philosopher and educational administrator.

Gustave Belot was born 7 August 1859 at [[Strasbourg]],<ref name=AP>''Archives de psychologie'', Vol. 23, 1932, p.77</ref> the son of a professor in the faculty of letters at [[Lyon]]s. He entered the [[École Normale Supérieure]] in 1878, taking the philosophy ''[[agrégation]]'' in 1881, and becoming a provincial philosophy instructor at [[Brest, France|Brest]] and elsewhere.<ref name=Gardet>Mathias Gardet,''Histoire des PEP: pupilles de l'école publique'',Editions Beauchesne, 2008, p.175</ref> In 1899 he succeeded [[Lucien Lévy-Bruhl]] as professor of philosophy at the [[Lycée Louis-le-Grand|''lycée'' Louis-le-Grand]]. In 1911 he was appointed Inspector of the Paris Academy, and in 1913 he became Inspector-General of Secondary Instruction.<ref name=Chimisso>Cristina Chimisso, ''Writing the history of the mind: philosophy and science in France, 1900 to 1960s'', Ashgate Publishing, Ltd., 2008, p.43</ref> He died in [[Paris]] on 21 December 1929.<ref name=AP/> Informed by [[Durkheimian]] sociology, he was a theorist of collectively-held 'lay morality', which was rational and based upon general interest.

Gustave Belot is the brother of [[Émile Belot]] (1857-1944), engineer, inventor and astronomer, and the great great uncle of [[Pierre Pincemaille]] (1956-2018), musician and organist.
