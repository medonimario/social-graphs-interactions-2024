'''Ángel Cappelletti''' (1927– November 25, 1995<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A0yjDwAAQBAJ&dq=%22Ángel+Cappelletti%22&pg=PT276|title=Venezuelan Anarchism: The History of A Movement 1811-1998|author=Rodolfo Montes De Oca|publisher=See Sharp Press|year=2019|isbn=9781947071377 }}</ref>) was a philosopher and university professor. He was born in [[Rosario, Santa Fe|Rosario]]. He studied philosophy at the [[Universidad Nacional de Buenos Aires]] where he also received his PhD in 1954. He moved to Venezuela in 1968 and began teaching at the [[Simón Bolívar University (Venezuela)|Simon Bolivar University]] until his retirement in 1994, when he returned to Rosario. During his career he translated works from Greek and Latin and composed numerous works on ancient philosophy as well as on the subjects as [[positivism]] and [[anarchism]] in Latin America.
