'''Jonathan Francis Bennett''' (17 February 1930 – 31 March 2024) was a philosopher of [[philosophy of language|language]] and [[metaphysics]], specialist of [[Immanuel Kant|Kant]]'s philosophy and a historian of [[early modern philosophy]]. He had New Zealand citizenship by birth and had since acquired UK and Canadian citizenship.

==Life and education==
Jonathan Bennett was born in [[Greymouth]], New Zealand to Francis Oswald Bennett and Pearl Allan Brash Bennett.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Hull|first=Richard|title=Jonathan Francis Bennett|journal=The American Philosophical Association Centennial Series|year=2013|volume=10|series=Presidential Addresses of The American Philosophical Association 1981–1990|pages=515–518|doi=10.5840/apapa2013172}}<!--|accessdate=13 December 2013--></ref> His father was a doctor and his mother a homemaker. He read philosophy at the [[University of Canterbury]] (formerly Canterbury University College)<ref name="UC">{{cite web|title=University of Canterbury, philosophy, Master's Theses|url=http://canterbury.libguides.com/content.php?pid=40483&sid=317727|publisher=University of Canterbury|access-date=13 April 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130505064559/http://canterbury.libguides.com/content.php?pid=40483&sid=317727|archive-date=5 May 2013}}</ref> and was awarded his MA there in 1953.<ref>{{cite thesis |last=Bennett |first=J.F. |year=1952 |type=Masters thesis |title=The paradoxes of strict implication |publisher=UC Research Repository, University of Canterbury |hdl=10092/13728 |doi=10.26021/4451 |url=https://ir.canterbury.ac.nz/handle/10092/13728}}</ref> He then went to the [[University of Oxford]] where he was a member of [[Magdalen College, Oxford]]. He obtained his [[Bachelor_of_Philosophy#University_of_Oxford|BPhil]] in 1955.

==Career==
Bennett's first academic post was as a junior lecturer at the [[University of Auckland]] (then Auckland University College), New Zealand (1952).<ref>{{cite journal|last=Hull|first=Richard|title=Jonathan Francis Bennett|journal=The American Philosophical Association Centennial Series|year=2013|volume=10|series=Presidential Addresses of The American Philosophical Association 1981–1990|pages=515|doi=10.5840/apapa2013172}}<!--|accessdate=13 December 2013--></ref> He was an instructor in philosophy at [[Haverford College]] (Pennsylvania) (1955–56), then a lecturer in moral science (philosophy) at the [[University of Cambridge]] (1956–68), then at [[Simon Fraser University]] (1968–70), the [[University of British Columbia]] (1970–79), and in 1979 he went to [[Syracuse University]] as professor of philosophy. He remained in this position until his retirement in 1997.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Levey|first1=Samuel|editor1-last=Brown|editor1-first=Stuart|title=Dictionary of Twentieth Century British Philosophers|date=2005|publisher=Thoemmes Press|isbn=184371096X|page=78|chapter=Bennett, Jonathan Francis (1930-)}}</ref>

In 1980, he was the Tanner Lecturer at [[Brasenose College]] of the [[University of Oxford]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Bennett|first=Jonathan|title=Who is Jonathan Bennett?|url=https://www.earlymoderntexts.com/faqs/bennett |work=earlymoderntexts.com |access-date=5 April 2024}}</ref> His lectures were refined and published in his 1995 book ''The Act Itself''. In this work he argues that letting someone die is as immoral as killing someone. This also applies to other harms that one commits or fails to prevent. This view has been widely discussed, for example by [[Judith Jarvis Thomson]].<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Thomson|first1=Judith Jarvis|title=Review: The Act Itself. by Jonathan Bennett|journal=[[Noûs]] |year=1996|volume=30|issue=4|pages=545–557|doi=10.2307/2216119|jstor=2216119}}</ref>

In 1992, he was the [[John Locke Lectures|John Locke Lecturer]] at the University of Oxford, giving lectures on 'Judging Behaviour: Analysis in Moral Theory'.<ref>{{cite web|title=The John Locke Lectures|url=http://www.philosophy.ox.ac.uk/john-locke-lectures|website=Faculty of Philosophy, University of Oxford|access-date=7 July 2017}}</ref> 

In 1985, he was elected a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]].<ref name=AAAS>{{cite web|title=Book of Members, 1780–2010: Chapter B|url=http://www.amacad.org/publications/BookofMembers/ChapterB.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|access-date=15 June 2011}}</ref> The [[British Academy]] extended him the same honour in 1991.<ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Jonathan Bennett |url=http://www.britac.ac.uk/users/professor-jonathan-bennett|website=British Academy|access-date=7 July 2017}}</ref> In the same year he was awarded a [[LittD]] from the University of Cambridge.

Bennett wrote extensively on [[philosophy of mind]], [[philosophy of language]], [[Event (philosophy)|events]], [[Strict conditional|conditional]]s, and [[consequentialism|consequentialist ethics]]. He was renowned for his interpretations of major early modern philosophers and wrote five books in this area.<ref>{{cite book |author=Lowe, E. J. |editor=Honderich, Ted |title=The Oxford Companion to Philosophy |url=https://archive.org/details/oxfordcompaniont00hond_585 |url-access=limited |publisher=Oxford University Press |location=Oxford |year=2005 |page=[https://archive.org/details/oxfordcompaniont00hond_585/page/n108 87] |isbn=0-19-926479-1 }}</ref> A [[Festschrift]] to commemorate his 60th birthday was published in 1990.<ref>{{cite book|editor1-last=Cover|editor1-first=J.A.|editor2-last=Kulstad|editor2-first=Mark |title=Central themes in early modern philosophy : essays presented to Jonathan Bennett |date=1990 |publisher=Hackett |location=Indianapolis|isbn=0872201090}}</ref>

Bennett's website was devoted to making the texts of early modern philosophers more accessible to today's students.<ref>{{cite web |title=Early Modern Texts |first=Jonathan |last=Bennett |url= https://www.earlymoderntexts.com/ |work=earlymoderntexts.com |access-date=5 April 2024}}</ref>

Bennett died aged 94 on 31 March 2024.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Leiter |first1=Brian |author-link=Brian Leiter |title=In Memoriam: Jonathan Bennett (1930-2024) |url=https://leiterreports.typepad.com/blog/2024/04/in-memoriam-jonathan-bennett-1930-2024.html |website=Leiter Reports |date=3 April 2024 |access-date=4 April 2024}}</ref>
