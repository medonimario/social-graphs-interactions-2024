'''Rafael Gambra Ciudad''' (21 July 1920 – 13 January 2004) was a Spanish philosopher, a [[secondary education]] official, a [[Carlist]] politician and a soldier. In [[philosophy]] he is considered key representative of late [[Traditionalism (Spain)|Traditionalism]]; his works fall also into [[Philosophy of Politics|theory of state and politics]]. He is best known as author of books focusing on secularisation of Western European culture in the [[consumer society]] era. As a politician he is acknowledged as a theorist rather than as an active protagonist, though after 2001 he briefly headed one of the Carlist branches.

==Family and youth==
[[File:José Ciudad (Cropped).jpg|thumb|160px|left|[[:es:José Ciudad Aurioles|José Ciudad]], maternal grandfather]]
Rafael's paternal ancestors for generations have been related to [[Valle de Roncal]]; until today Casa Gambra and Casa Sanz are iconic mansions of the area.<ref>compare ''Qué visitar'' section at the Ayuntamiento de Roncal web page, available [http://www.roncal.es/es/visitenos/visitar/ here] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160601122837/http://www.roncal.es/es/visitenos/visitar/ |date=2016-06-01 }}, or ''Roncal'' section at the ''vallederoncal'' tourist service, available [http://vallederoncal.es/discover/villages/roncal/?lang=en here]</ref> The Gambras made their name fighting the French in [[Peninsular War|1809]].<ref>Rafael Gambra, ''El Valle de Roncal en la Guerra de la Independencia'', [in:] ''Príncipe de Viana'' 20/76-77 (1959), pp. 187-215</ref> Rafael's grandfather, [[Pedro Francisco Gambra Barrena]] (died 1930),<ref>''ABC'' 25.05.30, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1930/05/25/055.html here]</ref> married descendant to a distinguished Carlist military Sanz family;<ref>Pedro Francisco Gambra y Barrena, a lawyer from Roncal, married Josefa Sanz y Escartín, the sister of [[:es:Eduardo Sanz y Escartín|Eduardo Sanz y Escartín]] and the cousin of [[Cesáreo Sanz Escartín]]</ref> himself he rose to high positions in Ministry of Economy.<ref>in the late 1870s he was jefe de administracion honorario employed in Dirección General de Propiedades y Derechos de Estado, see ''Guía Oficial de España'' 1879, p. 642, available [http://hemerotecadigital.bne.es/issue.vm?id=0000899508&page=642 here]. In the late 1880s he served as administrador de contribuciones in the province of Madrid, see ''La Iberia'' 28.05.89, available [http://hemerotecadigital.bne.es/issue.vm?id=0001658433&page=2 here], becoming Delegado de Hacienda in Valladolid in 1896, see ''La Iberia'' 28.01.96, available [http://hemerotecadigital.bne.es/issue.vm?id=0001774135&page=3 here]. In 1911 he rose to debt and budget administration in the ministry, ''Guía Oficial de España'' 1911, p. 514, available [http://hemerotecadigital.bne.es/issue.vm?id=0001003698&page=514 here]</ref> His son and Rafael's father, [[:es:Eduardo Gambra Sanz|Eduardo Gambra Sanz]] (1878-1964),<ref>''ABC'' 11.08.64, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1964/11/08/132.html here]</ref> became an architect.<ref>''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''[[Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco]]'' service, available [http://www.fnff.es/Rafael_Gambra_gran_filosofo_tradicionalista_672_c.htm here]</ref> Key Gambra's works are [[:es:La Gran Peña|offices of Sociedad Gran Peña]] along the [[Gran Vía (Madrid)|Gran Vía]]<ref>see drafts and sketches at ''europeana'' service, available [https://www.europeana.eu/portal/search?q=gambra here]</ref> and refurbishment of [[Palace of marqués de Miraflores|Palacio del Marqués de Miraflores]],<ref>and the Omnia building at Plaza de Colón, [[Ignacio Hernando de Larramendi y Montiano|Ignacio Hernando de Larramendi]], ''Así se hizo Mapfre. Mi tiempo'', Madrid 2000, {{ISBN|9788487863875}}, pp. 29-30</ref> marked by attempt to re-capture the splendor of historical Spanish architecture.<ref>''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''[[Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco]]''</ref> In 1915<ref>''ABC'' 10.02.15, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1915/02/10/017.html here]</ref> he married Rafaela Ciudad Villalón (died 1947),<ref>''ABC'' 03.08.47, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1947/08/03/002.html here], some sources claim her name was Rafaela Ciudad Orioles, Hernando de Larramendi  2000, pp. 29-30</ref> born in [[Seville]]<ref>Hernando de Larramendi 2000, pp. 29-30</ref> though raised in [[Madrid]].<ref>''ABC'' 05.01.11, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1911/01/05/008.html here]</ref> She came from a distinguished family of civil servants; her father [[:es:José Ciudad Aurioles|José Ciudad Aurioles]]<ref>''ABC'' 10.02.15, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1915/02/10/017.html here]</ref> in the early 20th century was a [[Cortes Generales|Cortes]] deputy,<ref>see the official Cortes service, available [http://www.congreso.es/portal/page/portal/Congreso/Congreso/SDocum/ArchCon/SDHistoDipu/SDIndHistDip?_piref73_1340024_73_1340023_1340023.next_page=/wc/servidorCGI&CMD=VERLST&BASE=DIPH&FMT=DIPHXLDA.fmt&DOCS=1-25&DOCORDER=FIFO&OPDEF=Y&QUERY=%2826270%29.NDIP. here]</ref> until the early 1920s a longtime [[Senate (Spain)|senator]],<ref>see the official Senate service, available [http://www.senado.es/web/conocersenado/senadohistoria/senado18341923/senadores/fichasenador/index.html?id1=728 here]</ref> and in 1917-1923 served as President of [[Supreme Court of Spain|Tribunal Supremo]].<ref>''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''[[Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco]]''</ref> The couple had only one child.<ref>''ABC'' 03.08.47, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1947/08/03/002.html here]</ref>

Born and raised in Madrid, Rafael spent much of his childhood in Valle de Roncal and later cherished his [[Navarre]]se heritage; in historiography he is referred to as a navarro rather than as a madrileño, sometimes dubbed "maestro navarro", "arquetipo navarro", "buen navarro" or "vasco-navarro roncalés".<ref>''Boletín del Círculo Tradicionalista Carlista San Mateo'' 39 (1998), ''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org'' service, available [http://filosofia.org/ave/001/a088.htm here], José de Armas, ''In memoriam. Rafael Gambra. Fidelidad a los principios y lealtad a las personas Rafael Gambra en mi personal "Camino de Damasco"'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), p.170</ref> He was brought up in profoundly Catholic ambience;<ref>compare his recollections ''Al Dios – y a la Iglesia – que alegraron mi juventud'', [in:] ''El Pensamiento Navarro'', issue from 1973, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1021808 here]</ref> politically his father sympathized with Carlism<ref>''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''[[Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco]]''</ref> and his mother, though coming from a Liberal family, also displayed a conservative penchant.<ref>''ABC'' 05.01.11, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1911/01/05/008.html here]</ref> He was first educated at the [[:es:Colegio Nuestra Señora del Pilar|Madrid Marianist Colegio del Pilar]]; already during his schoolboy years he was attracted to letters and read books while his colleagues played football;<ref>Pablo Larraz Andía, Víctor Sierra-Sesumaga (eds.), ''Requetés. De las trincheras al olvidio'', Madrid 2011, {{ISBN|9788499700465}}, p. 367</ref> during his early adolescent years he was engaged in [[:es:Asociación Católica Nacional de Propagandistas|Asociación Católica Nacional de Propagandistas]].<ref>Miguel Ayuso Torres, ''In memoriam. Rafael Gambra. Rafael Gambra en el pensamiento tradicional español'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), p. 163. When in his 50s Gambra appreciated the role of Acción Católica, though not that of CEDA, Tyre and Bloque Nacional, see Miguel Ayuso Torres, ''Koinos: el pensamiento politico de Rafael Gambra'', Madrid 1998, {{ISBN|9788473440424}}, p. 33, Jacek Bartyzel, ''Nic bez Boga, nic wbrew tradycji'', Radzymin 2015, {{ISBN|9788360748732}}, p. 105</ref>
[[File:Roncal from above.JPG|thumb|Valle de Roncal, view from upper part of the [[:es:Valle de Belagua|Belagua section]]]]
In July 1936 the Gambras were spending their summer holidays in Roncal, where they were caught by the [[Spanish coup of July 1936|military coup]].<ref>[[Onésimo Díaz Hernández]], ''Rafael Calvo Serer y el grupo Arbor'', Valencia 2011, {{ISBN|9788437087351}}, p. 43</ref> As a 16-year-old Rafael volunteered to the [[Requeté]] unit of Tercio de Abárzurza,<ref>Manuel Santa Cruz [[Alberto Ruiz de Galarreta Mocoroa|Alberto Ruiz de Galarreta]], ''Rafael Gambra. un hombre cabal'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), p. 174</ref> in few days time taking positions at [[Guadarrama Pass|Alto de León pass]]<ref>Gambra's own account of fightings on the Alto de León pass in the summer of 1936 ignores his own role, compare Rafal Gambra, ''1936: El Alto de Leon'', [in:] ''Siempre P'alante'' 1993, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022358 here]; for pro-Republican account of the same war episode see Mariano Maroto García, ''El 18 de julio en Leganes (II)'', [in:] ''Ciudadanas y Ciadadanos por el cambio'' 31.07.11, available [http://www.ignaciodarnaude.com/textos_diversos/El%2018%20de%20julio%20de%201936%20en%20Leganes(II),M.Maroto%20Garcia.pdf here]</ref> and attempting to break through [[Sierra de Guadarrama]].<ref>Luis Hernando de Larramendi, ''In memoriam. Rafael Gambra. Los Gambra y los Larramendi: una mistad carlista'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), p. 172</ref> José Ulíbarri, the Catholic parish priest from [[Yerri|Úgar]] and temporary commander of the unit, remained Gambra's friend and sort of mentor for life.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 175</ref> He spent the next 2 years at the stationary frontline in the Sierra, until in July 1938 he left to attend alférez provisional training.<ref>[[Julio Aróstegui]], ''Combatientes Requetés en la Guerra Civil española, 1936-1939'', Madrid 2013, {{ISBN|9788499709758}}, p. 389</ref> In February 1939 he was seconded to Tercio del Alcázar,<ref>a battalion composed mostly of the Castilians, for its operational history see Aróstegui 2013, p. 653-667</ref> commanding a [[platoon]] in the 4. Infantry Company.<ref>Aróstegui 2013, p. 667</ref> Having reached [[Lliria]] at the moment of Nationalist victory,<ref>''Ajencia Patriótica de Noticias de Acción Juvenil Española'' 13.01.04</ref> he was decorated with many military awards.<ref>Medalla de la Campaña 1936-1939, Cruz Roja del Mérito Militar, Cruz de Guerra and Medalla de Voluntarios de Navarra, ''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''[[Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco]]'', ''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref>

Rafael Gambra was married to [[:es:María del Carmen Gutiérrez Sánchez|María del Carmen Gutiérrez Sánchez]] (1921-1984), translator, scholar<ref>she was professor of history and geography at Instituto Ramiro de Maeztu in Madrid, ''Miguel Arazuri'', [in:] ''BiblioRomance'' service, available [http://www.biblioromance.es/Miguel-Arazuri.php here]</ref> and as Miguel Arazuri author of fairly popular novels.<ref>she authored some 30 novels, some of them forming part of a wider entity, see ''Miguel Arazuri'', [in:] ''BiblioRomance'' service, available [http://www.biblioromance.es/Miguel-Arazuri.php here]</ref> She was also the founder and manager of Fundación Stella, an independent radio venture.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 176</ref> The couple had three children.<ref>Larramendi 2004, p. 172, ''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> Of their two sons, [[Andrés Gambra Gutiérrez]] is professor of medieval history<ref>in UCM, UNED, Universidad Europea de Madrid, Colegio Universitario Domingo de Soto (Segovia), Universidad Francisco (Vitoria) and Universidad Rey Juan Carlos, see ''D. Andrés Gambra Gutiérrez'', [in:] ''Universidad Rey Juan Carlos'' service, available [https://www.urjc.es/universidad/organizacion#secretario-general here]; for some of his works see ''dialnet.unirioja'' service, available [https://dialnet.unirioja.es/buscar/documentos?querysDismax.DOCUMENTAL_TODO=andres+gambra here]</ref> and the university official,<ref>secretario general and member of Consejo de Gobierno, ''D. Andrés Gambra Gutierrez'', [in:] ''Universidad Rey Juan Carlos'' service, available [https://www.urjc.es/universidad/organizacion#secretario-general here]</ref> while [[José Miguel Gambra Gutiérrez]] is scholar in philosophy,<ref>see ''Profesores del Departamento'', [in:] ''Universidad Complutense, Departamento de Lógica y Filosófia de la Ciencia'' service, available [https://www.ucm.es/dpto_logica/profesores here]; for some of his works see ''dialnet.unirioja'' service, available [https://dialnet.unirioja.es/buscar/documentos?querysDismax.DOCUMENTAL_TODO=jose+miguel+gambra here]</ref> both in Madrid. The two are active Traditionalists,<ref>see e.g. Andrés Gambra, acting as official at Universidad Rey Juan Carlos, in public confronting marginalisation of Christianity and deification of progress, ''La Razón'' 15.02.11, available [http://www.larazon.es/historico/el-poder-politico-influye-demasiado-en-la-educacion-RJLA_RAZON_59070#.Ttt184c4QcqORxM here]</ref> the latter leading the sixtinos Carlists since 2010.<ref>see e.g. ''José Miguel Gambra, nuevo jefe de la Sec. Política de S.A.R. Don Sixto Enrique Bornón'', [in:] ''Radio Cristianidad'' service 09.03.10, available [https://radiocristiandad.wordpress.com/2010/03/09/jose-miguel-gambra-nuevo-jefe-de-la-sec-politica-de-s-a-r-don-sixto-enrique-de-borbon/ here]</ref>

==Scholarship==
[[File:Instituto Lope de Vega (Madrid) 01.jpg|thumb|left|[[Instituto Lope de Vega]], Madrid]]
In 1939 Gambra enrolled at the faculty of letters and philosophy at [[:es:Universidad de Madrid|Universidad Central]].<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> Influenced by [[:es:Manuel García Morente|Manuel García Morente]] and [[Salvador Minguijón Adrián]],<ref>see Rafael Gambra, ''Aspectos del pensamiento de Salvador Minguijón'', [in:] ''Revista international de sociologia'' 67 (1959), pp. 85-88</ref> he graduated in 1942.<ref>''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco''</ref> One year later he entered Cuerpo de Catedráticos Numerarios de Institutos Nacionales de Enseñanza Media.<ref>Ministerio de Educacion Nacional, ''Cátedra 1960-1961. Prontuario del Profesor'', Madrid 1960, p. 1021</ref> Promoted to Inspector Nacional de Enseñanzas Medias,<ref>María Teresa López del Castillo, ''La inspección del bachillerato en España (1845-1984)'', Madrid 2000, {{ISBN|9788436241457}}, p. 314</ref> in 1945 Gambra obtained his PhD laurels as Doctor en Filosofía, his thesis dedicated to post-Hegelian approach to historiographic methodology.<ref>titled ''La interpretación materialista de la historia (una investigación social-histórica a la luz de la filosofía actual)''</ref> The work, promoted by [[:es:Juan Zaragüeta|Juan Zaragüeta y Bengoechea]], boiled down to highly critical review centred on [[Karl Marx|Marx]] and [[Feuerbach]]<ref>see ''Sumarios y extractos de las Tesis Doctorales leídas desde 1940 a 1950 en las secciones de Filosofía y Pedagogía, Facultad de Filosofía y Letras, Universidad de Madrid'', pp. 61-66</ref> and was published in 1946.<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref>

Already in the early 1940s Gambra assumed teaching at the Madrid Academia Vazquez de Mella, a semi-official Carlist educational and cultural enterprise; he was giving lectures on Traditionalist theory of philosophy, state and politics.<ref>Hernando de Larramendi 2000, pp. 59, 89-60, Larramendi 2004, p. 172</ref> In 1943 he moved to [[Pamplona]], where he was employed by [[Instituto Príncipe de Viana]],<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 175, Onésimo Díaz Hernández, ''Rafael Calvo Serer y el grupo Arbor'', Valencia 2011, {{ISBN|9788437087351}}, p. 43</ref> a cultural outpost of Carlism managed by the provincial authorities. During the next 12 years Gambra served in the Institute as professor of philosophy,<ref>Díaz Hernández 2011, p. 561</ref> in the early 1950s apparently hoping to join a would-be Universidad del País Vasco-Navarro, a high-education establishment advocated at the time.<ref>Díaz Hernández 2011, p. 320</ref> However, when [[Universidad de Navarra]] materialized as a private [[Opus Dei]] enterprise in 1952, Gambra did not enlist; he rejected also an opportunity to pursue research and possibly scholarship in England.<ref>Díaz Hernández 2011, p. 361</ref> In the mid-1950s he returned to the capital,<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 175</ref> engaged in governmental attempt to re-shape the secondary education structures.<ref>Gambra was highly critical of hitherto organization of the secondary education, writing: "lo peor de estos centros es su constitución misma, esto es, la forma como están concebidos y organizados. La estructura decimonónica, individualista y meramente oficial (...) el orden existente en un instituto es meramente reglamentario o externo; fundamentalmente, la mera sujeción a un horario de asistencia a clase (...) Los institutos se hallan montados sobre la sagrada independencia de cátedra (...) El claustro como entidad de gobierno, casi inexistente en la actualidad, no entiende tampoco de asuntos internos pedagógicos o educativo", quoted after Ángel Lorente Lorente, ''Cincuenta años de regulación de las formas de coordinación docente en los institutos de educación secundaria'', [in:] Guillermo Vicente y Guerrero, (ed.), ''Estudios sobre la historia de la Enseñanza Secundaria en Aragón'', Zaragoza 2011, {{ISBN|9788499111759}}, pp. 402-403</ref> He assumed teaching in newly created "centros modelos de segunda enseñanza", first in [[Instituto Miguel de Cervantes]] and in the mid-1960s moving to [[Instituto Lope de Vega|Instituto Nacional de Enseñanza Media Lope de Vega]],<ref>''BOE'' 186 (1964), available [https://www.boe.es/boe/dias/1964/08/04/pdfs/A10151-10152.pdf here]</ref> becoming its vicedirector later on.<ref>''BOE'' 155 (1968), available [https://www.boe.es/boe/dias/1968/06/28/pdfs/A09495-09499.pdf here]; later the institute was renamed to Instituto Naciónal de Bachillerato Lope de Vega, ''BOE'' 284 (1977), available [https://www.boe.es/boe/dias/1977/11/28/pdfs/A26079-26085.pdf here]</ref> As education official he was anxious to prevent "erosión de la espiritualidad", and in the early 1960s he opposed technocratic changes, proposed and eventually introduced in education.<ref>Gambra claimed that "erosión de valores", and "erosión de la espiritualidad" might impair development and adversely affect equality of opportunities. Proponents of technocratic changes, J. A. Pérez Rioja and A. Abad, gained the upper hand, giving rise to the reform period dubbed "segunda sisactía", Emilio Castillejo Cambra, ''Mito, legitimación y violencia simbólica en los  manuales escolares de historia del franquismo (1936-1975)'', Madrid 2014, {{ISBN|9788436268645}} pp. 170, 671</ref> He co-operated also with [[Universidad Complutense]], particularly with the associated [[CEU San Pablo University|San Pablo CEU college]], run by ACdP. Gambra continued collaborating with CEU from the mid-1960s to 1994,<ref>exact nature of this collaboration is unclear. None of the sources consulted refers to Gambra as "academic"</ref> also after San Pablo had formally separated from Complutense and became an independent university.<ref>see cover info on Rafael Gambra, ''La Monarquia Social y Representativa en el pensamiento tradicional'', Madrid 2011, {{ISBN|9788435800426}}</ref> 
[[File:Sede de la AECID (Madrid) 01.jpg|thumbnail|240px|[[:es:Instituto de Cultura Hispánica|Instituto de Cultura Hispánica]]]]
Apart from professional engagements, Gambra continued his teaching career also with a number of semi-scholarly institutions. Until the early 1960s Gambra was giving lectures at [[Ateneo de Madrid]], at that time led by [[:es:Florentino Pérez Embid|Florentino Pérez Embid]],<ref>''ABC'' 30.11.55, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1955/11/30/036.html here], also ''ABC'' 18.12.59, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1959/12/18/065.html here]</ref> and [[:es:Instituto de Cultura Hispánica|Instituto de Cultura Hispánica]], an establishment created by Francoist authorities to cultivate the Latin American link.<ref>''ABC'' 17.11.66, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1966/11/17/087.html here], ''ABC'' 20.11.66, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1966/11/20/103.html here]</ref> In the press referred to as "catedrático",<ref>''La Vanguardia'' 04.09.65, available [http://hemeroteca.lavanguardia.com/preview/1965/09/04/pagina-5/33559928/pdf.html here]</ref> he was active in once-off conferences, also beyond Madrid,<ref>''ABC'' 11.03.55, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/sevilla/abc.sevilla/1955/03/11/025.html here], ''Ateneo Barcelones. Memoria. Curso de 1959-1960'', p. 5, available [http://prensahistorica.mcu.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=7&path=3031584&presentacion=pagina here]</ref> and in periodical Catholic cultural-scientific initiatives, like Conversaciones Intelectuales de El Paular<ref>''ABC'' 13.05.61, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/blanco.y.negro/1961/05/13/050.html here]</ref> or sessions organized by Hermandad Sacerdotal.<ref>''ABC'' 25.09.61, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/sevilla/abc.sevilla/1974/09/25/054.html here]</ref> In the mid-1960s he commenced engagement in Madrid-based [[Centro de Estudios Históricos y Políticos General Zumalacárregu]]i, a think-tank set up to disseminate Traditionalist thought and counter progressist designs on Carlism. In the late 1960s he stood as a recognized authority, at least within the Traditionalist realm.<ref>honored by homage sessions, ''ABC'' 09.07.68, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1968/07/09/069.html here]</ref> He continued giving lectures in the 1980s,<ref>e.g. on organization of secondary education, see ''ABC'' 11.04.84, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1984/04/11/033.html here]</ref> as late as 1989<ref>e.g. at Asociación Cultural Hispania, ''ABC'' 17.11.89, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1989/11/17/058.html here]</ref> and 1992.<ref>''ABC'' 11.02.92, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1992/02/11/048.html here]</ref> In 1998 he celebrated 50 years of teaching.<ref>''ABC'' 24.11.98, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1998/11/24/056.html here]</ref>

In terms of general inspiration Gambra is broadly defined as fitting within the [[Plato]]nic tradition<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 143</ref> but indebted mostly to [[St. Thomas Aquinas]] and occasionally referred to as member of the neo-scholastic school.<ref>Gabriel de Armas, ''Rafael Gambra y la unidad católica de España (I)'', [in:] ''Verbo'' 39 (1965), p. 533, also Pedro Carlos González Cuevas, ''La crisis del tradicionalismo carlista'', [in:] Pedro Carlos González Cuevas, Ana Martínez Arancón, Juan Olabarría Agra, Gabriel Plata Parga, Raquel Sánchez García, Javier Zamora Bonila (eds.), ''Historia del pensamiento político español. Del renacimiento a nuestros días'', Madrid 2016, {{ISBN|9788436271041}}, page unavailable, see [https://books.google.com/books?id=GXnCCwAAQBAJ&dq=doctrinario+rafael+gambra&pg=PT454 here]</ref> His views on Christianity were influenced by [[Gustave Thibon]],<ref>González Cuevas 2016</ref> [[Etienne Gilson]], [[Romano Guardini]] and partially [[Max Scheler]].<ref>de Armas 1965, p. 533</ref> He is also often referred to as embracing philosophic threads of [[Albert Camus]] and other French [[Existentialism|existentialists]],<ref>González Cuevas 2016</ref> while in theory of politics and state following [[Alexis de Tocqueville]], [[Karl von Vogelsang]]<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 143</ref> and especially [[Juan Vázquez de Mella]].

The principal thread of Gambra's thought is repudiation of rationalism-based civilization<ref>Miguel Ayuso Torres, ''El tradicionalismo de Gambra'', [in:] ''Razón española'' 89 (1998), p. 305</ref> and promotion of a Traditionalist outlook. Human life is understood as commitment to divine order<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 306</ref> and human identity as spanned between own self and community belonging.<ref>de Armas 1965, p. 553, González Cuevas 2016</ref> A man is perceived primarily as a social – not autonomous<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, pp. 309-310</ref> – being, expressed mostly by his role in society; similarly, life is about contributing to common good, incompatible with individualism or liberalism.<ref>also, Sartre and St. Exupery, Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 306</ref> The society itself is governed by nature, animality and rationality, though religion as a transcendent factor is indispensable element of social equation.<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 307</ref> Such a polity is best expressed as a "society of duties", united by common purpose and religious inspiration.<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 307-8</ref> As he believed a public organization not based on accepted orthodoxy can never be stable, leading to mere co-existence rather than community,<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 308, Bartyzel 2015, p. 95</ref> Gambra advocated public embracement of the doctrine<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 307. He opposed the 1967 ''Ley de la Libertad Religiosa'' as opening the gates to "penetración protestante y judaica", Juan Antonio Monroy, ''Un protestante en la España de Franco'', Madrid 2015, {{ISBN|9781496404244}}, page unavailable, see [https://books.google.com/books?id=m2DhBwAAQBAJ&dq=franquista+gambra&pg=PT142 here]. He perceived it as bowing to invasion of progressism and Europeisation, coming from Italy, France and the Vatican - Santa Cruz 2004, p. 176</ref> with respect for privately held heterodoxy.<ref>de Armas 1965, p. 553, Bartyzel 2015, p. 278</ref> 
[[File:Marcel Lefebvre 1981b.jpg|thumb|160px|[[Marcel Lefebvre]] C.S.Sp.,<ref>for a sample of Gambra's highly sympathetic though not openly anti-Vatican rebellious views on Lefebvre see e.g. ''Algo más sobre Monseñor Lefebvre'', [in:] ''La Nación'' 14.06.95, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1021916 here]</ref> picture from 1981]]
According to Gambra social self of a human is best expressed by tradition, viewed as accumulated and irreversible evolution that provides a principle governing historical societies<ref>at times this concept was applied to unlikely cases like a family saga from Colorado, screened on Spanish TV in the early 1980s, see Carmelo López-Arias Montenegro, ''In memoriam. Rafael Gambra. Rafael Gambra y el sentido del tiempo'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), p. 168</ref> and incompatible with revolutionary patterns of change.<ref>Juan B. Vallet de Goytisolo, ''In memoriam. Rafael Gambra. La luz que agradezco a Rafael Gambra'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), p. 180</ref> In case of Spain the tradition is embodied in hereditary monarchy as opposed to elective heads of state,<ref>he viewed elective leadership of state as hostage to ideocracy, Bartyzel 2015, pp. 132-3</ref> federative structure<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, pp. 310-311, Manuel Martorell Pérez, ''La continuidad ideológica del carlismo tras la Guerra Civil'' [PhD thesis in Historia Contemporánea, Universidad Nacional de Educación a Distancia], Valencia 2009, pp. 407-9, Bartyzel 2015, p. 127</ref> as opposed to unitary nation states,<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 139, González Cuevas 2016</ref> [[Corporatism|organic representation]]<ref>Ayuso Torres 1998, pp. 310-311</ref> as opposed to corruption-prone and individual-centred parliamentarian democracy,<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 138</ref> Catholic orthodoxy as opposed to secular or neutral regime<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 74</ref> and generally withdrawn admin structures as opposed to modern, omnipotent state.<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 140, Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 404</ref> Politically the guardian of such a tradition<ref>according to another summary, for Gambra the essence of Traditionalism was: 1) society as community united by common orthodoxy, 2) family as basis; 3) corporative purpose-guided structure (jerarquización teleológica); 4) subsidiarity principle of all public power; 5) organic representation, Bartyzel 2015, p. 74</ref> was Carlism,<ref>Miguel Ayuso Torres, ''El carlismo y su signo (a los 175 años)'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 14 (2008), p. 131</ref> not merely political grouping or romantic sentiment but rather the very essence of Spanish self.<ref>Ayuso Torres 2008, p. 132</ref>

Recurring thread of Gambra's thought, by some considered its key component,<ref>González Cuevas 2016</ref> was confronting modern format of religiosity. He held [[Maritain]] and [[Teilhard de Chardin]] responsible for undermining Christianity<ref>de Armas 1965, p. 555, Bartyzel 2015, p. 284</ref> and turning it into a "new humanist religion",<ref>see e.g. Rafael Gambra, ''Maritain y Teilhard de Chardin'', [in:] ''Verbo'' 78-79 (1969), p. 781, referred after Bartyzel 2015, p. 285</ref> admitting defeat in 150-year-old struggle<ref>in case of Spain commencing in the 1821-1823 war, Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 311, Bartyzel 2015, p. 58</ref> against secular Revolution.<ref>Jordi Canal, ''El carlismo'', Madrid 2000, {{ISBN|8420639478}}, p. 406; not to mention against Maurras, considered "tradicionalismo de izquierdas", González Cuevas 2016</ref> Fiercely critical of [[Vaticanum II]]<ref>Ayuso Torres 2004, pp. 163-4, Santa Cruz 2004, p. 177; he later referred to the Council as ''Los heraldos del anticristo'', see ''Boletín de Comunión Católico-Monárquica'' 11-12 (1985), available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1021923 here]</ref> he considered ''[[Dignitatis humanae]]'' incompatible with tradition,<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 289,  Mercedes Vázquez de Prada, ''El final de una ilusión. Auge y declive del tradicionalismo carlista (1957-1967)'', Madrid 2016, {{ISBN|9788416558407}}, p. 271</ref> the innovative effort of Vatican producing demolition of Christianity<ref>Francisco Javier Caspistegui Gorasurreta, ''El naufragio de las ortodoxias: el carlismo, 1962-1977'', Pamplona 1997, {{ISBN|9788431315641}}, pp. 148-150</ref> and reduction of Catholic integrity to mere "Christian inspiration".<ref>López-Arias Montenegro 2004, pp. 167-8</ref> Indeed, he is often referred to as an [[Integralism|Integrist]].<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 475, Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, p. 155. Gambra has not rejected the authority of Vatican; disillusioned about Paul VI, he noted that in John Paul I's public statements he heard about discipline, a word long forgotten in Vatican, and about faith instead of social problems, see ''El Imparcial'', available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022260 here]. Cautiously optimistic prior to the 1982 John Paul II's visit to Spain - see e.g. ''Siempre p'alante'' from 1982, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022486 here], he assumed a decisively skeptical stance in the late 1990s. He was anxious about ecumenical activity of the pope, see e.g. ''Fuerza Nueva'' 1997, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022014 here] or noted that while John Paul II in public hailed integrity of Italian state and spoke against separatism of right-wing Lega Nord, he has never supported Spanish territorial integrity and has never spoke against the left-wing Basque separatism, see ''Fuerza Nueva'' 1996, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022094 here]</ref> Faced with progressist stance of the Church, Gambra started assuming anti-clerical positions from the far-right perspective.<ref>e.g. in ''El Pensamiento Navarro'' he lambasted clergymen for systematically turning sermons into subversive political lectures, apparently with no reaction on part of official ecclesiastical authorities, referred after ''Mediterráneo. Prensa y radio del Movimiento'' 23.03.75, available [http://prensahistorica.mcu.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=13&path=5078613&presentacion=pagina here]. Gambra's views on cardenal [[Vicente Enrique y Tarancón|Tarancón]] were extremely critical and he did not refrain from mocking the head of Spanish church in public, compare an article with already abusive title ''La 'cana al aire' del cardenal Tarancón'', [in:] ''Fuerza Nueva'' 06.08.77, available [http://www.larramendi.es/i18n/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022305 here]</ref> He often combined his critique with onslaught on European idea, considered a euphemism denoting a militant, anti-Christian ideology,<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 89</ref> and opposed its implementation in Spain.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, pp. 175-6</ref> He was not that much anti-democratic as rather opposing deification of democracy,<ref>see e.g. his ''La democracia como religión'', [in:] ''Roma'' 89 (1985), available [http://adelantelafe.com/la-democracia-como-religion/ here]</ref> and especially the central if not exclusive position it claimed within public space.<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 207</ref>

==Works==
[[File:Romería de Villacisla-1.jpg|thumbnail|left|220px|[[Romeria|Romería]], Spain]]
Most popular Gambra's works were textbooks in [[history of philosophy]]: ''Historia sencilla de la filosofía'' (1961) and ''Curso elemental de filosofía'' (1962); tailored for the beginners, they were reprinted in countless editions and served as immensely popular introductions to philosophy for generations of Spanish students<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 162</ref> until the early 21st century.<ref>''Historia sencilla'' was published by Rialp, related to Opus Dei, ''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''; some counted him among opusdeístas, Gregorio Morán, ''El Maestro en el Erial: Ortega y Gasset y la Cultura Del Franquismo'', Madrid 1998, {{ISBN|9788483100493}}, p. 125. ''Historia sencilla'' enjoyed 25 re-editions, while ''Curso elemental'' had 17 re-runs</ref>  The first edition of ''Curso'' was nominally co-authored by [[Gustavo Bueno]]; in the subsequent re-runs the publisher dropped Bueno, who after the fall of Francoism claimed that the result of his own labor had been merely re-edited by Gambra.<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> In 1970 these works were supplemented by ''La filosofía católica en el siglo XX''.<ref>186 pages, available online at ''funcacionspeiro'' service [http://www.fundacionspeiro.org/verbo/1970/V-83-P-167-186.pdf here]</ref>

Gambra's view on theory of society and state was laid out in three works: his [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] dissertation ''La interpretación materialista de la historia'' (1946), ''La monarquía social y representativa en el pensamiento tradicional'' (1954)<ref>254 pages, available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=3&presentacion=pagina&path=1022100 here]</ref> and ''Eso que llaman estado'' (1958).<ref>229 pages, available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1021853 here]</ref> ''La monarquía'', together with almost simultaneously published similar work of [[Francisco Elías de Tejada y Spínola|Elías de Tejada]],<ref>and titled ''La monarquía tradicional'', 1953</ref> became a cornerstone of Traditionalist theoretical vision, while ''Eso que llaman'' was widely discussed in the press of the era;<ref>see e.g. ''ABC'' 01.05.58, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1958/05/01/054.html here]</ref> both earned Gambra prestigious standing in scholarly discourse.

The works which made most impact among the wide public were 4 books focusing on contemporary culture: ''La unidad religiosa y el derrotismo católico'' (1965),<ref>190 pp, Rafael Gambra: ''La unidad religiosa y el derrotismo católico. Estudio sobre el principio religioso de las sociedades históricas y en particular sobre el Catolicismo en la nacionalidad española'', Sevilla 1965, available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022432 here]</ref> ''El silencio de Dios'' (1967),<ref>202 pp, available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&presentacion=pagina&posicion=1&path=1022249 here]</ref> ''Tradición o mimetismo'' (1976)<ref>320 pp, available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022299 here]</ref> and ''El lenguaje y los mitos'' (1983).<ref>247 pp, available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022336 here]</ref> The first two centred on secularization of Western polities; they confronted Christian-Democratic vision<ref>detailed discussion in de Armas 1965</ref> and Vatican II alike,<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 163-4, Santa Cruz 2004, p. 177</ref> explored roots of perceived cultural decline, tried to re-define tradition versus progress<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> and strove to demonstrate how multifold advances of the last centuries have given man a false sense of mastery.<ref>Oskar Gruenwald, ''The promise of inter-disciplinary studies: re-imagining the university'', [in:] ''Journal of Interdisciplinary Studies'' 26 (2014), pp. 2-4</ref> ''Tradición'' turned dramatically out of tune with the [[Spanish transition to democracy|transición]] spirit, withdrawn by the editor and distributed by the author himself.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 178, Goytisolo 2004, p. 179</ref> Finally, ''El lenguaje'' deconstructed modern communication; its objective was to prove that the progressist tide manipulated the language and turned it from means of communication into means of promoting cultural revolution.<ref>with particular attention paid to terms like "democracy", "equality", "evolution", "progress", "humanism", "human rights" etc, Ayuso Torres 1998, p. 305, Bartyzel 2015, p. 207. ''El lenguaje y los mitos'' provided a counter-reference to another book on public communication, issued a few years earlier and having been a lecture of [[media manipulation]], elevated to the status of art and science; the book was fathered by former Gambra's disciple and Carlist collaborator turned his enemy, Ramón Massó, ''De la magia a la artesanía: el politing del cambio español'' (1980)</ref>
[[File:La Maquinista.jpg|thumbnail|220px|shopping mall, Spain]]
Apart from translations,<ref>Bertrand de Jouvenel, ''Du Pouvoir. Histoire naturelle de sa croissance'' (1956)</ref> booklets,<ref>e.g. ''Los tres lemas de la sociedad futura'' (1953)</ref> compilations<ref>e.g. collection of his earlier essays ''La moral existencialista'' (1955)</ref> and single though some of them original historical attempts,<ref>especially his ''Valle de roncal'' (1955, 71 pages) enjoyed a few re-editions, see online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?posicion=3&presentacion=pagina&path=1021839 here]. Far less popular was a compilation ''La Cristianización de América'' (1992), available online at ''Fundación Larramendi'' service [http://www.larramendi.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=1&presentacion=pagina&path=1022148 here]. The work which attracted scholarly discussion was ''La primera guerra civil de España 1821-23'' (1950), referred to as "catastrophic interpretation of Trienno Liberal as conflict between the peasants and the liberals", Ignacio Peiró Martín, ''Días de ayer de la historiografía española. La Guerra de Independencia y la "conversión liberal" de los historiadores en el franquismo'', [in:] Pedro Rújula López, Jordi Canal i Morell (eds.), ''Guerra de ideas: política y cultura en la España de la Guerra de la Independencia'', Madrid 2011, {{ISBN|9788492820641}} p 458</ref> most of 775 titles in digitalized version of Gambra's writings, released in 2002, are contributions to reviews and daily press.<ref>Larramendi 2004, p. 173</ref> Since the 1940s Gambra was moderately engaged<ref>Gambra published 6 articles in ''Arbor'' and is referred to as "colaborador ocasional", Díaz Hernández 2011, pp. 43, 351</ref> in [[Rafael Calvo Serer|Calvo Serrer]] directed ''[[:es:Arbor|Arbor]]''.<ref>Sara Prades Plaza, ''España y su historia. La generación de 1948'', Madrid 2014, {{ISBN|9788415444442}}</ref> Later he switched to ''La Ciudad Católica'', turned into ''[[Verbo (Spanish Christian review)|Verbo]]'', unofficial review of the Spanish Integrists,<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 177</ref> contributing also to other Catholic titles like ''Tradición Católica'' or conservative ones like ''Ateneo''. He supplied a number of Carlist reviews and bulletins:<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 164</ref> ''Siempre p'alante'', ''La Santa Causa'', ''[[Montejurra (magazine)|Montejurra]]'' and ''Azada y Asta'', gradually eradicated from the last two by their progressist management.<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 445, Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, p. 165</ref> Through many decades he was key author of ''[[:es:El Pensamiento Navarro|El Pensamiento Navarro]]''.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 175</ref> During late Francoism and afterwards he collaborated with ''El Alcázar'' and ''Fuerza Nueva''. In the 1990s and later he appeared in nationwide press mostly as author of letters to the editor;<ref>e.g. see his voice on veterans, ''ABC'' 08.06.92, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1992/06/08/046.html here], or on Pedro Almodovar, ''ABC'' 08.11.95, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1995/11/08/016.html here]</ref> his last item identified is from 2003.<ref>''ABC'' 26.08.95, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/2003/08/26/010.html here]</ref> In the 1950s he engaged in private Carlist ventures Editorial Cálamo<ref>Larramendi 2004, p. 172; the publishing house was the first one in the Francoist Spain to refer to Picasso with respect, Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 446</ref> and Ediciones Montejurra.<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 263</ref>

==Carlist: confronting Franco==
[[File:Entrevista entre Franco y el conde Ciano en San Sebastián en julio de 1939.jpg|thumb|160px|left|early Francoism]]
Released from the Carlist Requeté unit, during academic years in Madrid Gambra engaged in the Traditionalist Academia Vázquez de Mella.<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, pp. 203-5</ref> In the increasingly fragmented realm of post-war Carlism, headed by vacillating and hardly contactable<ref>first trapped in Vichy France and after mid-1944 detained by Gestapo in Dachau</ref> regent [[Prince Xavier of Bourbon-Parma|Don Javier]], he seemed leaning towards a candidature of [[Duarte Nuno, Duke of Braganza|Dom Duarte Nuño]]; the two had an amicable interview in 1941,<ref>[[Francisco Manuel de las Heras y Borrero|Francisco de las Heras y Borrero]], ''Un pretendiente desconocido. Carlos de Habsburgo. El otro candidato de Franco'', Madrid 2004, {{ISBN|8497725565}}, p. 41, Bartyzel 2015, p. 248</ref> but the young Gambra stayed within the limits of loyalty to the regent<ref>see his 1940 correspondence, Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, p. 18</ref> and eventually abandoned his pro-Braganza penchant,<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 248</ref> converted to supporter of the Borbón-Parmas.<ref>especially following dynastic reading presented by Fernando Polo in his ''¿Quién es el Rey?'', Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 395</ref> Having moved to Navarre in 1943 he assisted the French monarchists from fleeing the [[Nazi occupation of France|Nazi terror]] across the [[Pyrenees]] into Spain.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 175</ref> In the late 1940s he gained weight within Navarrese Carlism; in the early 1950s he was already listed among "dirigentes locales"<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 344</ref> who "tenían en el país vasconavarro una indudable influencia";<ref>Javier Lavardin [José Antonio Parilla], ''Historia del ultimo pretendiente a la corona de España'', Paris 1976, p. 15</ref> in 1953 he formally entered Junta Provincial.<ref>Mercedes Vázquez de Prada Tiffe, ''El papel del carlismo navarro en el inicio de la fragmentación definitiva de la comunión tradicionalista (1957-1960)'', [in:] ''Príncipe de Viana'' 72 (2011), p. 395</ref>

Since emergence of pro-Francoist [[Carloctavismo|Carloctavista]] branch in the mid-1940s Gambra was increasingly anxious about protracted regency of Don Javier.<ref>Bartyzel 2015, pp. 248-9</ref> Uncompromising towards another collaborative branch, the [[Tomás Domínguez Arévalo|Rodeznistas]],<ref>in the early 1950s he demanded that collaborative Carlists are made to stand clear what they are for, Vázquez de Prada Tiffe 2011, p. 395</ref> in the early 1950s he concluded that Don Javier should reinvigorate the movement by terminating the regency and declaring his own claim to the throne. When this eventually happened in 1952, Gambra was co-author<ref>together with [[Francisco Elías de Tejada y Spínola|Francisco Elías de Tejada]] and [[Melchor Ferrer Dalmau|Melchor Ferrer]]</ref> of ''Acto de Barcelona'', a proclamation issued by the pretender and viewed also as major re-definition of the Carlist dynastical reading.<ref>Mercedes Vázquez de Prada Tiffe, ''El nuevo rumbo político del carlismo hacia la colaboración con el régimen (1955-56)'', [in:] ''Hispania'' 69 (2009), p. 193, Bartyzel 2015, p. 251</ref> Books on Traditionalist monarchy established his firm position as a recognized party theorist. In 1954 he entered sub-commission of culture within Comisión de Cultura y Propaganda of the Carlist executive, Junta Nacional.<ref>Robert Vallverdú i Martí, ''La metamorfosi del carlisme català: del "Déu, Pàtria i Rei" a l'Assamblea de Catalunya (1936-1975)'', Barcelona 2014, {{ISBN|9788498837261}}, p. 138</ref>
[[File:Don Javier 1960.jpg|thumb|160px|[[Prince Xavier of Bourbon-Parma|Don Javier]], 1960]]
When discussing the mid-1950s some authors count him among the immovilistas, non-collaborative followers of [[Manuel Fal Conde|Manuel Fal]],<ref>Vázquez de Prada Tiffe 2009, p. 182</ref> while others scholars suggest he charged Fal with lack of intransigence and together with other Navarros like the [[Joaquín Baleztena Ascárate|Baleztena]] [[Ignacio Baleztena Ascárate|brothers]] he opposed him from even more anti-Francoist positions.<ref>Lavardin 1976, pp. 15, 18; others claim that differences were rather of personal nature, Ramón María Rodon Guinjoan, ''Invierno, primavera y otoño del carlismo (1939-1976)'' [PhD thesis Universitat Abat Oliba CEU], Barcelona 2015, p. 115</ref> Once Carlism changed its strategy towards cautious rapprochement, Gambra stuck to his guns and lambasted the official collaborationist path of the new leader, [[José María Valiente Soriano|José María Valiente]].<ref>for 1956 see Vázquez de Prada 2016, p. 67; for 1957 letter see Gambra's letter to Valiente, reading that "La política de acercamiento al régimen (o de acogida de un supuesto llamamiento del Generalísmo), que Ud. siempra ha propugnado, producirá, a mi juicio, [...] los siguentes efectos: 1) resultados políticos nulos; 2) situación de ridículo general ante el país; 3) desaliento, división y aun violencias graves entre los Carlistas", quoted after Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, p. 293, see also Vallverdú i Martí 2014, p. 157; for 1958 see Vázquez de Prada 2016, p. 78</ref>

Uneasy about continuous vacillation of Don Javier,<ref>for 1955 see Vázquez de Prada 2016, p. 33; in 1956 he edited a memorandum aimed against a would-be reconciliation with the Alfonsists, prepared in wake of rumors of Don Javier preparing such a deal, Vázquez de Prada 2016, pp. 37-38</ref> Gambra was cautiously supportive of inviting his son [[Carlos Hugo, Duke of Parma|Don Carlos Hugo]] into the Spanish politics; he first met him in 1955<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 263</ref> and though perplexed by unfamiliarity of the francophone prince with the Spanish affairs,<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 395, Lavardin 1976, p. 18, Bartyzel 2015, p. 265</ref> it was Gambra who introduced him at the 1957 Carlist gathering at [[Montejurra]].<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 416, Vázquez de Prada Tiffe 2011, p. 401, Lavardin 1976, p. 32, Vázquez de Prada 2016, p. 58</ref> In the late 1950s Gambra appreciated his energetic style and focus on dynastic loyalty; he also liked young personalities from Don Carlos Hugo's entourage,<ref>for good initial relations between Gambra and AET see Vázquez de Prada 2016, p. 54</ref> especially [[Ramón Massó Tarruella|Ramón Massó]], a former Gambra's disciple from Academia Vázquez de Mella. Appreciating him as a young Catalan easily communicating with the crowd,<ref>"primero habla [in Montejurra 1956] un estudiante catalán, que tiene el acierto de coger enseguida la onda de la emoción popular y de hablar con frases breves y rotundas. El público entra de maravilla, aplaude, interrumpe, completa y redondea las frases del orador", quoted after Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 385</ref> at the turn of the decades Gambra collaborated with Massó and others;<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 382</ref> he did not realize that they exploited [[Mellismo|Mellist]] and federalist threads<ref>"La obra de Gambra sirvió de base teórica para quienes prepararon, adoctrinaron y lanzaron políticamente al primogénito de los Borbón Parma, Carlos Hugo", Martorell Pérez 2009, pp. 361, 397-409</ref> but considered him a rotten reactionary<ref>according to them, "Estas treinta o cuarenta páginas [of Gambra's work] sobran, no valen absolutamente nada", Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 412</ref> and approached his teaching highly selectively.<ref>Martorell Pérez 2009, p. 479-80</ref> It was only in the early 1960s that Gambra realized that [[Carlos Hugo, Duke of Parma|Huguistas]] tried to outsmart the Traditionalists; having failed to prevent their increased presence in the party structures, around 1963 Gambra dissociated himself from Carlohuguismo to launch an openly confrontational bid.

==Carlist: confronting Progress==
[[File:Jaarlijkse bijeenkomst van Carlisten op de Los Angeles berg bij Madrid.jpg|thumb|left|Carlist meeting, 1966]]
In 1963 Gambra co-founded Centro de Estudios Históricos y Políticos General Zumalacárregui;<ref>Vallverdú i Martí 2014, p. 230</ref> though affiliated with Movimiento Nacional,<ref>Jacek Bartyzel, ''Tradycjonalizm a dyktatura. Francisco Elías de Tejada y Spínola wobec frankizmu'', [in:] Marek Maciejewski, Tomasz Scheffler (eds.), ''Studia nad Autorytaryzmem i Totalitaryzmem'' 36/2 (2014), p. 7. In general, during the 1960s Carlism was tolerated as semi-legal grouping. When a formal case was lodged before the court by an Alfonsist monarchist, who indicated that Comunión Tradicionalista, noted in the press, is illegal organisation, the answer was as follows: "El Tribunal de Orden Público abrió el correspondiente sumario pero a la postre resolvió abstenerse de entrar en el fondo del asunto y archivar las actuaciones porque La Comunión Tradicionalista, según el Decreto d 19 de abril de 1937, integró con Falange Española una entidad de carácter nacional, en tanto se declaraban disueltas las demás organizaciones y partidos políticos, de donde se obtiene una legitimidad de origen y, por consiguiente, una imposibilidad de subsumir a dicha Comunión en las previsiones penales sobre asociaciones políticas, pues ni esta prohibida por la Ley ni ha dejado de cumplir los requisitos o trámites exigidos para constituirla. Añade que su actuación, como integrante del Movimiento y sometida a su régimen juridico, queda al margen de la Ley de Asociacines, por lo que cualquier irregularidad para poder ser estimada exigiria como requisito previo a modo de condición objetive de procedibilidad el que la misma fuese declarada por el propio Movimiento", quoted after Rodon Guinjoan 2015, p. 222-223</ref> it was intended as a think-tank disseminating Traditionalism and countering [[Partido Carlista|progressist]] vision of the Huguistas.<ref>Ayuso 2008, p. 20; at the time its chief exponent was [[José María Zavala Castella]], later joined by Pedro José Zabala and others</ref> Apart from publications and minor events,<ref>Centro issued also periodicals and organized so-called Jornadas Forales across the country, Bartyzel 2015, p. 265</ref> its activity climaxed in two Congresos de Estudios Tradicionalistas, staged in 1964 and 1968.<ref>Ayuso 2008, p. 20, Bartyzel 2014</ref> As supporters of Don Carlos Hugo were gaining momentum,<ref>starting mid-1960s the Javierista Montejurra feasts turned into massive public events, the largest in Spain save for official, religious or football gatherings. See the attendance numbers in Jeremy Macclancy, ''The Decline of Carlism'', Reno 2000, {{ISBN|9780874173444}}, p. 275: initially attracting few thousand attendants, in the early 1960s they attracted over 50,000 and in the mid-1960s over 100,000</ref> Gambra was leaning towards rapprochement among all Carlist branches separated from the party during the last 20 years: Rodeznistas, Carloctavistas, [[Maurici de Sivatte i de Bobadilla|Sivattistas]] and the recently expulsed politicians like [[José Luis Zamanillo González-Camino|José Luis Zamanillo]] or Francisco Elías de Tejada.<ref>Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, pp. 182-3</ref> Parking dynastical issues,<ref>Gambra was somewhat reluctant to openly challenge the aging Don Javier and tended to consider him incapacitated by Don Carlos Hugo, Ayuso 2004, p. 163</ref> they were supposed to be united by loyalty to Traditionalist principles and opposition to socialist penchant.<ref>Bartyzel 2015, p. 266</ref> The plan did not work out until the early 1970s, it is until emergence of ephemeral structure styled as ex-Requeté organization.<ref>in 1972, Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, pp. 231-2</ref>

Gambra's efforts of the time were mostly about further refinement of Traditionalist thought in specialized reviews and conferences;<ref>organized by Catholic, Carlist, conservative and finally post-Francoist groupings: Ciudad Católica, Jornadas por la Unidad Católica, Homenaje a Mella, Centro Zumalacárregui, Patronato de Fundación Elías de Tejada, Priorato de la Hermandad de San Pío X, Movimiento Católico Español, Fuerza Nueva</ref> they climaxed in ''¿Qué es el carlismo?'' (1971), concise lecture of the doctrine co-authored with de Tejada and [[:gl:Francisco Puy Muñoz|Puy Muñoz]]. Also in the mid-1970s he was very engaged in propaganda war with the Huguistas. The latter lambasted his accord with "ultra-fascist line" of ''El Pensamiento Navarro''<ref>''Información Española'' 16.09.70, available [http://prensahistorica.mcu.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=8&path=3090288&presentacion=pagina here]</ref> and his "posición ultramontana";<ref>''Información mensual'' 14 (1971), available [http://prensahistorica.mcu.es/es/catalogo_imagenes/grupo.cmd?interno=S&posicion=4&path=3107568&presentacion=pagina here]</ref> Gambra mobilized Traditionalists to challenge progressist grip on Carlism and prior to the [[Montejurra massacre|1976 Montejurra gathering]], which effectively produced fatal casualties, called for "asistencia masiva de los verdaderos tradicionalistas, que acallará gestos y voces, 'declaraciones' y 'manifiestos', sencillamente inadmisibles, intolerables".<ref>''Por qué hay que reconquistar Montejurra'', [in:] ''El Pensamiento Navarro'' 04.05.76, quoted after Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, pp. 343-4</ref>

During final years of Francoism and during transición Gambra, always opposed to Falangism and Francoism,<ref>"Gambra fue filósofo y escritor tradicionalista, siempre partidario firme de la línea no colaboracionista" - Vázquez de Prada Tiffe 2009, p. 187. According to one scholar, Gambra was the only firmy anti-Francoist among the 4 key Traditionalist theorists of the era, as Elías de Tejada moved from enthusiastic supporter to opposition, d'Ors has always been sympathetic and Canals displayed increasingly pro-regime penchant, Bartyzel 2015, p. 252</ref> neared the [[Búnker|post-Francoist búnker]]<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 163</ref> when confronting the change;<ref>starting the late 1960s Gambra criticised the regime for its increasingly liberal course, perceived as opening to laicism and inorganic democracy, see ''El Pensamiento Navarro'' 30.05.74, quoted after Caspistegui Gorasurreta 1997, p. 166. Following the fall of Francoism Gambra began to view the regime mostly in terms of etatism, bureaucracy, dirigisme and technocracy, with falangismo, sindicalismo, nacional-catolicismo etc merely ideological smokescreen cover-up, Bartyzel 2015, p. 261</ref> voicing against the [[Spanish Constitution of 1978|1978 constitution]],<ref>because the draft did not refer to God as supremr authority, Gambra with some 60 other pundits signed a letter calling to reject the project in a referendum, Bartyzel 2015, p. 294</ref> he sided with [[Blas Piñar|Blas Piñar's]] [[New Force (Spain)|Fuerza Nueva]] until the party was dissolved in 1982.<ref>''Rafael Gambra, gran filósofo tradicionalista'', [in:] ''Fundación Nacional Francisco Franco''</ref> Embittered by ongoing change he tried to confront it in newspapers;<ref>e.g. he campaigned against legalising divorce, ''ABC'' 14.10.80, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1980/10/14/052.html here]</ref> he compared the setting of late transición to that of the mid-1930s, when Spain was struggling infected with deadly political viruses.<ref>"hoy – en 1979 – nos encontramos ante un horizonte político tan sombrío como el que precidió el alzamiento de 1936", quoted after Canal 2000, p. 392</ref> He remained on the sidelines of politics; when mushrooming Traditionalist grouplets united in [[:es:Comunión Tradicionalista Carlista (1986)|Comunión Tradicionalista Carlista]] in 1986 Gambra focused rather on youth Carlist organizations,<ref>indeed a number of young people admitted having been captured by his arguments, also on TV, see de Armas 2004, Víctor Ibáñez, ''Rafael Gambra y las Juventudes Tradicionalistas'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 2004 (10), pp. 164-166, López-Arias Montenegro 2004</ref> his spotlight on cultural heritage and education.<ref>Ayuso 2004, pp. 164-6</ref> His objective was to promote Traditionalist values in the increasingly secular, modern consumer Spanish society. 
[[File:1999-01-21 SAR Sixte de Bourbon CTG.jpg|thumb|[[Prince Sixtus Henry of Bourbon-Parma|Don Sixto]], 1999]]
In the late 1980s and the early 1990s Gambra was considered supreme authority on theory of Traditionalism.<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> This was acknowledged during homage celebrations organized in 1998,<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 179</ref> though formal recognition came 3 years later. In 2001 [[Prince Sixtus Henry of Bourbon-Parma|Don Sixto]], the younger son of Don Javier styled as Abanderado de la Tradición (though falling short either of claiming the throne or even claiming the Carlist regency) nominated Gambra chief of his Secretaría Política.<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 165</ref> Not all the Traditionalists recognized Gambra's authority; the Carloctavistas and Comunión Tradicionalista Carlista - not admitting allegiance to any dynastical line - dissociated themselves from the nomination.<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> Assuming political leadership of the sixtinos Carlists, the 81-year-old considered his elevation another cross to bear, though as an octogenarian Gambra remained fairly active; his last public appearance took place during the [[Cerro de los Ángeles]] feast in 2002.<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 165</ref>

==Reception and legacy==
[[File:Flag of Cross of Burgundy.svg|thumbnail|left|Carlist standard]]
Gambra emerged as a notable Traditionalist theorist in the mid-1950s. In the late 1960s he was already a recognized authority within the movement and beyond,<ref>e.g. he featured in Juan Rodríguez Ruiz, ''Traditionalism'', [in:] ''Enciclopedia de la Cultura Española'', Madrid 1968, vol. 5, pp. 456-459</ref> with first homage sessions organized in 1968;<ref>''ABC'' 09.07.68, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1968/07/09/069.html here]</ref> at that time his books on culture and Christianity earned him name also among wider national audience. With the commencing transición of the late 1970s his works went out of fashion up to the point of withdrawing them from bookstores.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 178, Goytisolo 2004, p. 179</ref> Following the 1983 publication of ''El lenguaje y los mitos'' he started to disappear from public discourse, publishing either in specialized reviews or partisan press titles.<ref>like ''Siempre p'alante'' or ''Fuerza Nueva''</ref> Within Traditionalism he grew to an iconic figure, honored by 1998 homage session<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 179</ref> and a monograph by [[:es:Miguel Ayuso|Miguel Ayuso]], published the same year.<ref>Miguel Ayuso Torres, ''Koinós: el pensamiento político de Rafael Gambra'', Madrid 1998, {{ISBN|9788473440424}}</ref> Though he gained a number of prizes, all of them were awarded by conservative institutions.<ref>Premio Patronato Olave (1949), Premio Vedruna (1965), Premio Víctor Pradera (1973), Premio de Fundación Oriol-Urquijo (1975), Premio Manuel Delgado Barreto (1996)</ref> His 2004 death was noted by some though not all nationwide newspapers<ref>e.g. ''ABC'' 14.01.04, available [http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/2004/01/14/045.html here]; the Barcelona ''La Vanguardia'' did not acknowledge his death</ref> and acknowledged by monographic issue of ''Anales de Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada''.<ref>see online version available at ''Fundación Elias de Tejada'' service, available [http://fundacioneliasdetejada.org/anales/ano-x-2004/ here]</ref>

[[Taxonomy (general)|Taxonomy]] of Gambra's work is unclear. Today he is usually vaguely referred to as "thinker",<ref>"pensador", see e.g. Pedro Carlos González Cuevas, ''Tradicionalismo'', [in:] Javier Fernández Sebastián (ed.), ''Diccionario político y social del siglo XX español'', Madrid 2008, {{ISBN|9788420687698}}, p. 1172. Some authors almost explicitly admit difficulty in getting Gambra classified when they refer to him as "pensador tradicionalista en un sentido amplio", Edualdo Forment, ''El pensamiento cristiano'', [in:] Manuel Garrido, Nelson R. Orringer, Luis M. Váldes, Margarita M. Váldes (eds.), ''El legado filosófico español e hispanoamericano del siglo XX'', Madrid 2009, {{ISBN|9788437625973}}, p. 422, also "myśliciel" in Bartyzel 2015, p. 16</ref> an application used also by the Spanish press during his lifetime, alternating with "publicista", "catedrático", "profesor" or "ensayista".<ref>see e.g. Francisco Vega Oncins (ed.), ''Gran Enciclopedia de España'', vol. 9, Zaragoza 1993, {{ISBN|8487544096}}, p. 4389</ref> When it comes to detailed designation his work is usually classified as philosophy,<ref>see e.g. Javier Castro-Villacañas, ''El fracaso de la monarquía'', Madrid 2013, {{ISBN|9788408036678}}, p. 47, Robert A. Herrera, James Lehrberger, Melvin Eustace Bradford (eds.), ''Saints, sovereigns, and scholars: studies in honor of Frederick D. Wilhelmsen'', Virginia 1993, {{ISBN|9780820419299}}, pp. xxi, Alexandra Wilhelmsen, ''La formación del pensamiento político del carlismo, 1810-1875'', Madrid 1995, {{ISBN|9788487863318}}, p. 108, Danilo Castellano, ''Patrie Regioni Stati e il processo di unificazione europea'', Roma 1999, {{ISBN|9788881148134}}, p. 108</ref> though some see it as part of historiography<ref>Canal 2000, p. 422</ref> or political science.<ref>González Cuevas 2011, González Cuevas 2016</ref> In general Spanish encyclopedias he usually merits very brief notes.<ref>compare e.g. very brief entry in Oncins 1993, p. 4389; apart from basic facts from his biography, the note lists his major works but does not spare a single word on what his thought was all about</ref> Some philosophy-dedicated dictionaries ignore him,<ref>see e.g. a 4-volume series of José Ferrater Mora, ''Diccionario de filosofía'', vol. 2, Barcelona 2004, {{ISBN|8434405024}}</ref> some just mention his name<ref>Heleno Saña, ''Historia de la filosófia española'', Madrid 2007, {{ISBN|9788496710986}}, p. 255; Gambra is mentioned in the chapter titled "Franco-falangismo y nacional-catolicismo" as one of many authors whose principal aim was to provide "politico-philosophical coverage" to "Gloriosa Cruzada Nacional"; he is specified as belonging to "sector más especificamente católico"</ref> and in few he is treated at length.<ref>Forment 2009, pp. 422-424</ref> In some manuals he appears in entries dealing with Traditionalism, though featured as a secondary theorist who failed to provide original contribution and was rather a renovator<ref>"básicamente una renovación de los supusestos de Vázquez de Mella", González Cuevas 2016</ref> of earlier thought.<ref>"actualizar las teorías de Vázquez de Mella", González Cuevas 2008. p. 1171</ref> As a historian by some he is denied sound credentials, counted among pseudo-scientific "historiografia neotradicionalista",<ref>which demonstrated "scientific appearance based on erudition and sort of positivist illusion", Canal 2000, p. 422</ref> others challenge this approach.<ref>Alfonso Bullón de Mendoza, ''Jordi Canal, El Carlismo'' [review], [in:] ''Aportes'' 45 (2001), p. 155; wider discussion also in Alfonso Bullón de Mendoza, ''La parcialidad de los historiadores españoles'', [in:] John Vincent, ''Introducción a la Historia para gente inteligente'', Madrid 2013, {{ISBN|9788497391351}}, pp. 9-38</ref> As education official he is considered opponent of modernising technocratic changes.<ref>Castillejo Cambra 2014, pp. 170, 671</ref> He is sometimes counted among members of "[[generación de 1948]]".<ref>among Rafael Calvo Serer, Álvaro D'Ors, Francisco Elías de Tejada, Gonzalo Fernández de la Mora, Antonio Fontán, Hans Juretschke, Juan José López Ibor, Vicente Marrero, Vicente Palacio Atard, Florentino Pérez Embid, José Luis Pinillos and Jaume Vicens Vives, see Antoni Raja i Vich, ''El Problema de España bajo el primer franquismo, 1936-1956. El debate entre Pedro Laín Entralgo y Rafael Calvo Serer'', [PhD thesis Universitat Pompeu Fabra], 2010, p. 16</ref>
[[File:Montejurra San Cipriano.jpg|thumb|[[Montejurra]], 2014]]
Within Traditionalism Gambra remains one of the all-time greats. His opus is applauded as grand contribution,<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 163</ref> a synthesis<ref>Ayuso 2004, p. 162</ref> and a holistic "cosmovisión católica y española",<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 174</ref> though few authors consider his input an update rather than an original contribution.<ref>Ibáñez 2004, p. 164</ref> Gambra's followers see him as "pensador tradicionalista contemporáneo más importante",<ref>''Rafael Gambra. Tres crónicas'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 2004 (10), p. 159</ref> "esencia del tradicionalismo"<ref>López-Arias Montenegro 2004, p. 167</ref> or even "el más grande y el más fiel filósofo español de la segunda mitad del siglo XX",<ref>''Rafael Gambra Ciudad 1920-2004'', [in:] ''filosofia.org''</ref> himself and counter-revolution being one and the same thing.<ref>Santa Cruz 2004, p. 174</ref> However, also within the Traditionalist realm he found some of his concepts – especially his intransigence - challenged, like in case of a 2003 polemics with [[Álvaro d'Ors Pérez-Peix|Alvaro d'Ors]].<ref>Gambra replied to d'Ors in ''Quíen busca la destrucción del carlismo?'', [in:] ''Boletín Carlista de Madrid'', 70 (2003), referred after Bartyzel 2015, pp. 315-316, detailed discussion in Miguel Ayuso Torres, ''In memoriam. Álvaro D'Ors y el tradicionalismo (A propósito de una polémica final)'', [in:] ''Anales de la Fundación Francisco Elías de Tejada'' 10 (2004), pp. 183-197</ref> During the 2014 conference on Traditionalism<ref>titled ''Maestros del tradicionalismo hispánico de la segunda mitad del siglo XX''</ref> Gambra was dedicated one paper and featured prominently in a number of others; apart from that, both his sons acted as speakers.<ref>see the conference leaflet available [http://fundacioneliasdetejada.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/PROGRAMA-MAESTROS-DEL-TRADICIONALISMO.pdf here]</ref> In the 2015 synthesis of Traditionalist thought he is the second – after Elías de Tejada - most featured author.<ref>in Bartyzel 2015 Gambra is referred to 75 times</ref> Some of Gambra's essay books are being re-published, especially ''El Silencio de Dios'' enjoyed a number of editions and was translated into French<ref>''Le Silence de Dieu'', translated by Jacques Follon, Paris 2012, {{ISBN|9782360401161}}</ref> and English.<ref>''The Silence of God'', translated by Rebecca Vitz Cherico, Wilmington 2014, {{ISBN|9781610170970}}</ref> A new book was released posthumously.<ref>''El Exilio y el Reino. La comunidad de los hombres y sus enemigos'', Madrid 2009, {{ISBN|9788493664268}}</ref>
