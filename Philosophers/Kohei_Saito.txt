{{Nihongo|'''Kohei Saito'''|斎藤 幸平|Saitō Kōhei}} (born January 31, 1987)<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=koheisaito0131 |number=32187985231347712 |author=Kohei Saito |date=31 January 2011 |script-title=ja:誕生日だが |trans-title=it's my birthday |language=ja |access-date=2022-09-20}}</ref> is a Japanese [[philosopher]]. He is an [[associate professor]] at the [[University of Tokyo]]. Saito works on ecology and political economy from a [[Marxist]] perspective. His 2020 book ''[[Capital in the Anthropocene]]'' has been credited for inspiring a resurgence of interest in Marxist thought in Japan.<ref name="kyodo">{{Cite web |last=NEWS |first=KYODO |title=FEATURE: More young Japanese look to Marx amid pandemic, climate crisis |url=https://english.kyodonews.net/news/2021/05/ec42a541b32e-feature-more-young-japanese-look-to-marx-amid-pandemic-climate-crisis.html |access-date=2022-10-20 |website=Kyodo News+ |quote=The book's success has inspired a renaissance of interest in Marxist thought.}}</ref><ref name="guardian">{{Cite web |last=McCurry |first=Justin |date=2022-09-09 |title='A new way of life': the Marxist, post-capitalist, green manifesto captivating Japan |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2022/sep/09/a-new-way-of-life-the-marxist-post-capitalist-green-manifesto-captivating-japan |access-date=2022-10-20 |website=the Guardian |language=en |quote=... bookshop chains cleared space for special displays of revivalist Marxist literature.}}</ref> He is an advocate of [[degrowth]] communism.

== Early life and education ==
{{expand section|date=September 2024}}
Kohei Saito was born in Japan.{{where|date=September 2024}} He graduated from Shiba Junior & Senior High School (芝中学校・芝高等学校), a private school in Tokyo.{{when|date=September 2024}} Saito studied [philiosophy?] at the [[University of Tokyo]] (UTokyo) for three months{{when|date=September 2024}} and voluntarily withdrew from UTokyo. He enrolled in [[Wesleyan University]], [[Connecticut]] from 2005 to 2009 as a Freeman Asian Scholarship recipient.<ref>{{Cite web |last=斎藤 |first=幸平 |date=2022 |title=私の駒場とマルクス - 教養学部報 - 教養学部報 |url=https://www.c.u-tokyo.ac.jp/info/about/booklet-gazette/bulletin/638/open/638-03-3.html |access-date=2022-10-21 |website=www.c.u-tokyo.ac.jp |quote="同時に、私がアメリカに留学した際、ウェズリアン大学がくれた「フリーマン奨学金」には受給条件が一つだけありました。"}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |last=栄陽子 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bgUiIwUUdlsC |title=留学奨学金と節約術: アメリカ留学にかかる費用 |date=2008-01-25 |publisher=三修社 |isbn=978-4-384-02050-2 |pages=129 |language=ja}}</ref> In 2009, he began his master's studies at the [[Free University of Berlin]], and in 2012 he switched to [[Humboldt University of Berlin]] where he was enrolled until 2015.<ref name="faculty">{{Cite web |title=Kohei Saito |url=https://researchmap.jp/7000022985?lang=en |access-date=2022-07-07 |website=researchmap.jp |language=en}}</ref> Andreas Arndt was his doctoral advisor.{{cn|date=September 2024}} 

==Career==
Saito was subsequently{{when|date=September 2024}} a visiting scholar at the [[University of California, Santa Barbara]].{{cn|date=September 2024}}  In 2016, Saito published the book ''Nature versus Capital: Marx's Ecology in his Unfinished Critique of Capitalism,'' which is based on his doctoral dissertation. In it, he reconstructs [[Karl Marx]]'s ecological critique of capitalism, by drawing on the manuscripts and excerpts from Marx's estate.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.perlentaucher.de/autor/kohei-saito.html |title=Kohei Saito |work=perlentaucher.de |language=en |access-date=2022-07-07}}</ref> 

Saito co-edited Volume 18 of Division Four of the [[Marx-Engels-Gesamtausgabe]], which was published in 2019. Work on this book had informed his dissertation, which he completed at the [[Humboldt University of Berlin]].{{when|date=September 2024}} In 2017, the English-language version of his book was published. From 2017 to March 2022 he was an associate professor at [[Osaka Metropolitan University]].<ref name="faculty"/> He was made an associate professor at the University of Tokyo Graduate School of Arts and Sciences Interdisciplinary Cultural Studies in April 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.c.u-tokyo.ac.jp/info/research/faculty/newlyappointed/ics/f029241.html|title=齋藤 幸平（サイトウ コウヘイ） - 新任教員紹介|accessdate=20 April 2022|publisher=University of Tokyo Graduate School of Arts and Sciences|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20220417144050/https://www.c.u-tokyo.ac.jp/info/research/faculty/newlyappointed/ics/f029241.html|archivedate=17 April 2022|url-status=bot: unknown}}</ref>

==Work==
His 2020 published volume ''[[Capital in the Anthropocene]]'' became an unexpected [[bestseller]] in Japan, selling over 250,000 copies by May 2021,<ref name="kyodo"/> sales of which rose to more than 500,000 by mid-2022.<ref name=guardian/><ref>{{Cite web |last1=Schmelzer |first1=Matthias |last2=Vansintjan |first2=Aaron |date=19 September 2022 |title=Degrowth is not austerity – it is actually just the opposite |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2022/9/19/degrowth-is-not-austerity-it-is-actually-just-the-opposite |access-date=2022-10-20 |website=www.aljazeera.com |language=en}}</ref> In February 2023, Saito published ''Marx in the Anthropocene: Towards the Idea of Degrowth Communism'',<ref>{{cite web|access-date=2023-02-28|author=Maya Goodfellow|date=2023-02-28|language=en|title=A greener Marx? Kohei Saito on connecting communism with the climate crisis|url=https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2023/feb/28/a-greener-marx-kohei-saito-on-connecting-communism-with-the-climate-crisis|website=theguardian.com}}<!-- auto-translated by Module:CS1 translator --></ref> and the English translation of his "degrowth manifesto" was published in 2024.<ref>''Slow down. The degrowth manifesto'', Astra publishing, {{ISBN|9781662602368}}</ref>

==Awards==
In 2018, Saito won the [[Deutscher Memorial Prize]] for Marxist Research named after [[Isaac Deutscher]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://climateandcapitalism.com/2018/11/25/karl-marxs-ecosocialism-wins-the-2018-deutscher-prize/ |title='Karl Marx's Ecosocialism' wins the 2018 Deutscher Prize |work=Climate and Capitalism|date=2018-11-25 |language=en |access-date=2022-07-07}}</ref> as first Japanese and youngest person to receive the award.<ref>{{Cite web |title=斎藤幸平『大洪水の前に』待望の文庫化。発売即重版も決定！ |url=https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000011437.000007006.html |access-date=2022-10-26 |website=プレスリリース・ニュースリリース配信シェアNo.1｜PR TIMES |date=24 October 2022 |quote="斎藤幸平さんは、この『大洪水の前に』でマルクス研究者の最高権威ドイッチャー記念賞（2018年度）を日本人初・史上最年少（当時31歳）で受賞。"}}</ref> In 2020, he was awarded the JSPS Prize by  the [[Japan Society for the Promotion of Science]] for his book Capital in the Anthropocene.
