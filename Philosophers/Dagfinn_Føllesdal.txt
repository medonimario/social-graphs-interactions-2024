'''Dagfinn Føllesdal''' (born 22 June 1932) is a [[Norwegian_Americans|Norwegian-American]] [[philosopher]]. He is the [[Clarence Irving Lewis]] Professor of Philosophy Emeritus at [[Stanford University]], and professor emeritus at the [[University of Oslo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hf.uio.no/ifikk/english/people/aca/philosophy/emeriti/dagfinnf/index.html|title=Dagfinn Kåre Føllesdal - Department of Philosophy, Classics, History of Art and Ideas|website=www.hf.uio.no|access-date=2019-08-22}}</ref>

== Biography and career ==

Føllesdal was born in [[Askim]]. After earning his bachelor's and master's degrees at the [[University of Oslo]], he attended [[Harvard University]] and earned his Ph.D. in 1961 under [[Willard Van Orman Quine]]. He taught at [[Harvard University]] from 1961 to 1964, and began teaching at [[Stanford University]] in 1968.

Føllesdal is a member of the [[Norwegian Academy for Language and Literature]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.riksmalsforbundet.no/Det-Norske-Akademi.aspx |title=Det Norske Akademi for Sprog og Litteratur |publisher=Norwegian Academy for Language and Literature |language=Norwegian |access-date=25 April 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090210050543/http://www.riksmalsforbundet.no/Det-Norske-Akademi.aspx |archive-date=10 February 2009 }}</ref> the [[Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dnva.no/c26848/artikkel/vis.html?tid=27639|title=Gruppe 3: Idéfag|publisher=[[Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters]]|language=Norwegian|access-date=28 October 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927170302/http://www.dnva.no/c26848/artikkel/vis.html?tid=27639|archive-date=27 September 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kva.se/KVA_Root/eng/contact/searchcontacts/detail.asp?PersonID=1006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170912234735/http://www.kva.se/KVA_Root/eng/contact/searchcontacts/detail.asp?PersonID=1006|url-status=dead|archive-date=2017-09-12|title=The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences: Dagfinn Føllesdal|access-date=2009-05-01}}</ref> and the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]].<ref>{{Cite book|last=Gibson|first=Roger F.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Ijpj1tB3Qr0C&pg=PA829|title=Dictionary of Modern American Philosophers|date=2005|publisher=A&C Black|isbn=9781843710370|location=|pages=829|language=en|chapter=Føllesdal, Dagfinn Kaare (1932–)|author-link=Roger Gibson|chapter-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210312102630/https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/9780199754663.001.0001/acref-9780199754663-e-326}}</ref>

== Philosophical work ==

Føllesdal has written extensively on topics relating to the [[philosophy of language]], [[Phenomenology (philosophy)|phenomenology]], [[existentialism]], and [[hermeneutics]]. He was a pupil of Quine and is among the leading experts on the [[indeterminacy of translation]].
