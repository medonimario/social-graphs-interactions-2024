}}'''Felix Krueger''' (or Krüger) (10 August 1874 in Posen – 25 February 1948 in Basel) was a German [[psychologist]] and [[philosopher]]. He was a student of [[Wilhelm Wundt]] (who is regarded as the father of psychology). 

From 1912 to 1913 Krueger was an exchange professor at [[Columbia University]] and after returning to Germany in 1917, he succeeded Wundt at the University of Leipzig, where he founded the second Leipzig school of psychology, whose principles were based on a genetic psychology of wholeness and structure (genetische Ganzheits- und Strukturpsychologie). 

His most noteworthy achievement is "Das Wesen der Gefühle", Leipzig 1928, (translated as "The Essence of Feeling", Worcester, MA: Clark University Press, 1928)

In 1929 he belonged to the founding members of [[Alfred Rosenberg]]'s [[Militant League for German Culture]].<ref>George Leaman: ''Heidegger im Kontext: Gesamtüberblick zum NS-Engagement der Universitätsphilosophen'' (= ''Ideologische Mächte im deutschen Faschismus.'' Band 5). Argument, Hamburg 1993, {{ISBN|3-88619-205-9}}, p. 56.</ref>

When the Nazis seized power in 1933 Krueger wrote: “This is not only about the future of Germany. Ethics and thus the life of the white race are at stake.”<ref>George Leaman: ''Heidegger im Kontext: Gesamtüberblick zum NS-Engagement der Universitätsphilosophen'' (= ''Ideologische Mächte im deutschen Faschismus.'' Band 5). Argument, Hamburg 1993, {{ISBN|3-88619-205-9}}, p. 138. Original in German: "Hierbei geht es nicht nur um die deutsche Zukunft. Die Gesittung und mit ihr das Leben der weißen Menschheit steht auf dem Spiel."</ref>

The Nazi authorities eventually later found out that his grandfather was a "full-blooded [[Jew]]."<ref>Jerry Z. Muller, ''The Other God that Failed: Hans Freyer and the Deradicalization of German Conservatism'', Princeton University Press (1987), p. 284</ref>
