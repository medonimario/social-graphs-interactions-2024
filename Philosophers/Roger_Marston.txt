'''Roger Marston''' (Rogerus de Marston) (died c. 1303) was an English [[Franciscan]] scholastic philosopher and theologian.

He studied under [[John Pecham]] in Paris, in the years around 1270, and probably also at Oxford a few years later, during the time he was a pupil of [[John Pecham]] he was a fellow student with [[Matthew of Aquasparta]]. He generally followed Pecham's views on the [[Eucharist]].<ref>David Burr, ''Eucharistic Presence and Conversion in Late Thirteenth-Century Franciscan Thought'' (1984), pp. 57-8.</ref> He regarded time as absolute.<ref>Pasquale Porro, ''The Medieval Concept of Time: Studies on the Scholastic Debate and Its'' (2001), p. 201.</ref>

He became Franciscan Provincial in [[England]].<ref>[http://www.british-history.ac.uk/report.aspx?compid=40197 Friaries - The house of Grey Friars | British History Online<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>
