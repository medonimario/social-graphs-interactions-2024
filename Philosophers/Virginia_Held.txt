'''Virginia Potter Held''' (born October 28, 1929) is an American moral, social/political and [[feminist philosopher]] whose work on the [[ethics of care]] sparked significant research into the ethical dimensions of providing care for others and critiques of the [[Gender roles|traditional roles of women in society]].<ref name=iep>{{cite book|last=Saunder-Sault|first=Maureen|title=Care Ethics|year=2011|publisher=Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy|url=http://www.iep.utm.edu/care-eth/}}</ref><ref name=sep1>{{cite book|last=Tong, Williams|first=Rosemarie, Nancy|title=Feminist Ethics|year=2009|publisher=Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy|url=http://plato.stanford.edu/entries/feminism-ethics/}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-54896400.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140610193615/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-54896400.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2014-06-10|title=Norms and Values: Essays on the Works of Virginia Held.(Review)|last=Smiley|first=Marion|date=June 1999|work=American Political Science Review |via=[[HighBeam Research]]|access-date=December 30, 2013}}</ref>

==Beliefs==
Held defends care ethics as a moral framework distinct from [[Kantian ethics|Kantian]], [[Utilitarianism|utilitarian]] and [[virtue ethics]].<ref name=iep/> She holds that care is fundamental to human institutes and practices, indeed to our survival. Tong and Williams<ref name="sep1" /> quote: "There can be no ju[s]tice without care…for without care no child would survive and there would be no persons to respect."<ref>Virginia Held, The Ethics of Care 2006, p. 17</ref>

Held's work on the morality of political violence viewed through the window of ethics of care has also been significantly influential.<ref name=the>{{cite news|title=How Terrorism Is Wrong: Morality and Political Violence|url=http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/402933.article|access-date=June 25, 2013|newspaper=Times Higher Education}}</ref>

==Career==
Held was named Distinguished Professor at the [[City University of New York]] – Graduate Center and [[Hunter College]] in 1996.

She received her Ph.D. in philosophy from [[Columbia University]] in 1968 and worked at Hunter College as lecturer (1965–69), assistant professor (1969–72), associate professor (1973–77) and full professor from 1977 to her retirement in 2001. Held was affiliated with the [[CUNY Graduate Center]] in 1973, and served as deputy executive officer of the Philosophy program at the CUNY Graduate Center from 1980 to 1984. She also served as president of the Eastern Division of the [[American Philosophical Association]] in 2001–2002.<ref name=cv>{{cite web|last=Held|first=Virginia|title=Virginia Held Curriculum Vitae|url=http://web.gc.cuny.edu/philosophy/faculty/CVs/held_cv.pdf|access-date=June 25, 2013}}</ref>
