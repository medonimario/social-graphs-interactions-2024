'''Gwenaëlle Aubry''' (born 2 April 1971{{cn|date=May 2024}}) is a French [[novelist]] and [[philosopher]].

==Personal life and education ==
After two years of preparatory classes at the Lycée Henri-IV in Paris, Aubry began her studies at the [[Ecole Normale Supérieure|École Normale Supérieure]] in 1989 at the age of eighteen, earning an ''[[agrégation]]'' in Philosophy in 1992. She then received the Knox Scholarship at [[Trinity College, Cambridge]], where she earned a Master of Philosophy. In 1999, she received a Doctorate in Philosophy from the [[Paris-Sorbonne University|Université de Paris IV-Sorbonne]].<ref>{{Cite thesis|title=De l'en-puissance à la toute-puissance aspects de la puissance d'Aristote à Leibniz|url=http://www.sudoc.abes.fr/xslt/DB=2.1//SRCH?IKT=12&TRM=049474642&COOKIE=U10178,Klecteurweb,I250,B341720009+,SY,NLECTEUR+WEBOPC,D2.1,Edc232e72-11,A,H,R24.218.199.167,FY&COOKIE=U10178,Klecteurweb,I250,B341720009+,SY,NLECTEUR+WEBOPC,D2.1,E400300a6-11,A,H,R208.80.154.49,FY|publisher=Université Paris-Sorbonne|date=1998|place=1970-2017, France|degree=Thèse de doctorat|first=Gwenaëlle|last=Aubry}}</ref>

She is married to the philosopher [[Quentin Meillassoux]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=igvdCQAAQBAJ|title=Quentin Meillassoux|last=Harman|first=Graham|date=2015-01-12|publisher=Edinburgh University Press|isbn=9780748693474|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
She was an associate professor in ancient philosophy at the [[Nancy 2 University|Université de Nancy II]] from 1999 to 2002. She now serves as a Director of Research at the Centre national de la recherche scientifique [National Centre for Scientific Research]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://umr8230.vjf.cnrs.fr/?lang=fr|title=Centre Jean Pépin|website=umr8230.vjf.cnrs.fr|access-date=2019-04-22|archive-date=2019-04-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406185206/http://umr8230.vjf.cnrs.fr/?lang=fr|url-status=dead}}</ref> and an associate member of the Centre international d'étude de la philosophie française contemporaine [International Center for the Study of Contemporary French Philosophy] (ENS-Ulm).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://umr8230.vjf.cnrs.fr/spip.php?article26&lang=fr|title=AUBRY Gwenaëlle - Centre Jean Pépin|website=umr8230.vjf.cnrs.fr|access-date=2019-04-22|archive-date=2017-06-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170609104210/http://umr8230.vjf.cnrs.fr/spip.php?article26&lang=fr|url-status=dead}}</ref> She is also a member of the reading committee at the [[Théâtre national de la Colline]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.colline.fr/groupe-des-lecteurs|title=groupe des lecteurs {{!}} La Colline théâtre national|website=www.colline.fr|access-date=2019-04-22}}</ref>

==Her work ==
She published her first novel, ''Le Diable détacheur'', in 1999.

2002 saw the publication of ''L'Isolée'', which was inspired by [[Rey-Maupin affair|Florence Rey]], and then revised and expanded in 2003 with the addition of the short narrative ''L'Isolement.''

In 2007, after a residence at the [[Villa Medici]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.villamedici.it/fr/residences/gwenaelle-aubry/|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry|website=Villa Medici|language=fr-FR|access-date=2019-04-22}}</ref> she published ''Notre vie s'use en transfigurations'', excerpts of which were staged by Sarah Oppenheim for the play "Donnez-moi donc un corps!", put on in 2017 by the [[Théâtre du Soleil]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sceneweb.fr/donnez-moi-donc-un-corps-une-creation-du-bal-rebondissant-mise-en-scene-de-sarah-oppenheim/|title=Donnez-moi donc un corps ! Une création du Bal Rebondissant mise en scène de Sarah Oppenheim|last=presse|first=Dossier de|date=2017-01-19|website=Sceneweb|language=fr-FR|access-date=2019-04-22}}</ref>

In 2009, Aubry received the [[Prix Femina]] for ''Personne,'' an alphabet-novel, which is a portrait of a melancholic "from twenty-six angles with nothing at the center".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/livres/article/2009/11/09/gwenaelle-aubry-obtient-le-femina-avec-personne_1264622_3260.html|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry obtient le Femina avec "Personne"|author=Rousseau|first=Christine|date=9 November 2009|work=LE MONDE|access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lejdd.fr/Culture/Livres/Actualite/Gwenaelle-Aubry-Au-nom-du-pere-149067/|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry: Au nom du père|author=Marie-Laure Delorme|date=9 November 2009|work=Le Journal du Dimanche|access-date=15 March 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091112103013/http://www.lejdd.fr/Culture/Livres/Actualite/Gwenaelle-Aubry-Au-nom-du-pere-149067/|archive-date=12 November 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lepoint.fr/actualites-litterature/2009-09-24/puissante-gwenaelle-aubry/1038/0/379958|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry, puissante|author=Jean-Paul Enthoven|author-link=Jean-Paul Enthoven|date=2009-09-24|work=Le Point|access-date=2010-03-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100328095103/http://www.lepoint.fr/actualites-litterature/2009-09-24/puissante-gwenaelle-aubry/1038/0/379958|archive-date=2010-03-28|url-status=dead}}</ref> The book was also short-listed for the [[Prix Médicis]], the [[Grand Prix du roman de l'Académie française|Grand Prix du Roman de l'Académie française]], the Prix Novembre, and the [[Prix de Flore]]. It has been translated into a dozen languages and was published in the United States as ''No One'' in Trista Selous's translation with a preface by [[Rick Moody]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://frenchculture.org/books-and-ideas/2611-gwenaelle-aubry-rick-moody|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry in Conversation with Rick Moody|website=frenchculture.org|language=en|access-date=2019-04-22|archive-date=2019-04-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190422172301/http://frenchculture.org/books-and-ideas/2611-gwenaelle-aubry-rick-moody|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2012 ''Partages'' came out, a "book of hauntings", which mirrors, sometimes on alternating pages, the voices of two young girls, one Jewish, the other Palestinian, in Israel during the [[Second Intifada]]. It was long-listed for the [[Prix Goncourt]] and was a finalist for the Grand Prix du Roman de l'Académie française.

In 2015, she published "Lazare mon amour", a prismatic portrait of Sylvia Plath, in ''L'Une et L'Autre''. She then adapted it into a play, which was published separately in 2016.

In 2016 the autobiographical novel ''Perséphone 2014'' came out. Performed by the author and accompanied by the guitarist Sébastien Martel, the text was also staged by Anne Monfort on the occasion of the tenth Festival de Caves.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.festivaldecaves.fr/|title=Festival de Caves|access-date=2019-04-22}}</ref> Partial translations into English have appeared from Benjamin Eldon Stevens (''Arion'', winter 2018, 25.3: 161–173)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bu.edu/arion/archive/volume-25/|title=Volume 25 {{!}} Arion|website=www.bu.edu|access-date=2020-01-27}}</ref> and from Wendeline A. Hardenberg ([[Asymptote (journal)|''Asymptote'']], April 2019).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.asymptotejournal.com/fiction/gwenaelle-aubry-persephone-2014/|title=Persephone 2014 - Asymptote|website=www.asymptotejournal.com|language=en|access-date=2019-04-22}}</ref>

In 2018 her novel ''La Folie Elisa'' was published.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://next.liberation.fr/livres/2018/11/16/gwenaelle-aubry-scenes-de-rupture_1692621|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry, scènes de rupture|date=2018-11-16|website=Libération.fr|language=fr|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref> A staged version featuring Aubry, guitarist Sébastien Martel, and Judith Chemla was performed in November 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.maisondelapoesieparis.com/events/gwenaelle-aubry-la-folie-elisa/|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry – " La Folie Elisa "|website=www.maisondelapoesieparis.com|language=fr-FR|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref>

Aubry produced a radio play adaptation of [[Hermann Broch]]'s ''[[The Death of Virgil]]'' for [[France Culture]] and has written literary studies of [[Yves Bonnefoy]], [[W. G. Sebald]], and [[Georges Perec]], as well as short works published in magazines and journals, particularly ''[[Nouvelle Revue Française|La Nouvelle Revue française]]''. She is also the author of several books and a number of articles on [[ancient philosophy]] and its contemporary reception, as well as a translator from ancient Greek ([[Plotinus]], [[Porphyrus of Antioch|Porphyrus]], [[Proclus]]). In 2018 ''Genèse du Dieu souverain''. ''Archéologie de la puissance II'' was published, the second volume following ''Dieu sans la puissance''.<ref>{{Cite book|url=http://www.vrin.fr/book.php?code=9782711628063|title=Genèse du Dieu souverain|last=Aubry|first=Gwenaelle|language=fr|access-date=2019-04-22|archive-date=2021-08-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210802213354/https://vrin.fr/book.php?code=9782711628063|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cornell.edu/video/gwenaelle-aubry-genesis-violent-god|title=Genesis of the Violent God|website=CornellCast|language=en|access-date=2019-04-22}}</ref> The author rejected the [[Unmoved mover|first mover]] of [[Aristotle]] could ever have been the first principle of all things because it is solely able to attract entities capable of its imitation, and declared to choose the potence of all beings (in [[ancient Greek]]: [[Potentiality and actuality#Platonism and neoplatonism|dunamis pantōn]]) described by [[Plotinus]] and [[Plato]].<ref>{{cite journal|author=Lloyd P. Gerson|url=https://journals.openedition.org/philosant/2024?lang=en|title=Gwenaëlle Aubry, Genèse du Dieu souverain. Archéologie de la puissance II|doi=10.4000/philosant.2024|access-date=April 25, 2021|journal=Philosophie Antique|issue=20|year=2020|pages = 303–305|issn=2648-2789|oclc=	8861741761|doi-access=free}}</ref>
