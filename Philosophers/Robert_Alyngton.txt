'''Robert Alyngton''' (a.k.a. '''Arlyngton'''; died September 1398), was an [[English people|English]] [[philosopher]] who developed new [[logical]], [[semantic]], [[metaphysical]], and [[ontological]] theories in 14th century thought.  Alyngton is credited with creating the ideological foundation for the [[Oxford realism|Oxford Realists]] by substituting reference to objective reality with reference to mental and linguistic reality.<ref>{{cite SEP |url-id=alyngton |title=Robert Alyngton |last=Conti  |first=Alessandro|date=2007-10-14 }}</ref>

==Career==
Alyngton was a [[Fellow (college)|Fellow]] of [[The Queen's College, Oxford]]<ref name="wood">{{cite book |title= The History and Antiquities of the Colleges and Halls in the University of Oxford |via= [[Internet Archive]] | first=Anthony |last=Wood| chapter-url=https://archive.org/details/appendixtohistor00wood | chapter=Fasti Oxonienses| year=1790 |page=[https://archive.org/details/appendixtohistor00wood/page/34 34] }}</ref> from 1379 until 1386. He was deeply influenced by the [[metaphysics]] of [[John Wyclif]] who began his theological studies at Queen's College in 1363.  Alyngton was [[Chancellor (education)|Chancellor]] of [[Oxford University]] in 1394–5.<ref name="hibbert">{{cite book | title=The Encyclopaedia of Oxford | title-link=The Encyclopaedia of Oxford | publisher=[[Macmillan Publishers|Macmillan]] | chapter=Appendix 5: Chancellors of the University |year=1988 |pages=521–522 | isbn=0-333-39917-X | editor-last=Hibbert | editor-first=Christopher | editor-link=Christopher Hibbert}}</ref> He later became [[Rector (ecclesiastical)|Rector]] of [[Long Whatton]], [[Leicestershire]], until his death 1398.

==Philosophical works==
* ''Litteralis sententia super Praedicamenta Aristotelis'' — a commentary on Aristotle's Categories. This is Alyngton's most famous work.{{citation needed|date=July 2012}}
* ''Tractatus de suppositionibus terminorum'' — a treatise on the supposition of terms.  Early [[linguistic philosophy]].
* A commentary on the ''[[Liber sex principiorum]]''.
* ''Tractatus generum'' — a treatise on the genera of being.
