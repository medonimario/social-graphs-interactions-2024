'''Jerzy Wróblewski''' (5 September 1929 in [[Vilnius|Wilno]] - 31 May 1990 in [[Switzerland]])<ref>http://www.ktfp.eu/katedra/jerzy-wroblewski/prof-jerzy-wroblewski-english-version/ | Biography by Prof. M. Zirk</ref> was a Polish legal theorist and a professor at the [[University of Łódź]].

== Biography ==
Wróblewski graduated at the [[Jagiellonian University]] in 1947 with master's degree in [[law]] and completed postgraduate studies in [[philosophy]]. After obtaining his [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in 1949, he moved to the [[University of Łódź]] in 1951, where he was appointed head of chair of the theory of state and law. He achieved [[habilitation]] in 1970. He remained at Łódź till his death, where he performed a number of functions: pro-dean of the Faculty of Law (1953-1955, and 1958-1962), [[Dean (education)|dean]] of the Faculty of Law (1955-1956, and 1962-1964), pro-rector (1965-1968), and [[Rector (academia)|rector]] (1981-1984).

He served as a judge of the [[State Tribunal of the Republic of Poland]]. He was a member of the [[Polish Academy of Sciences]] and the [[Finnish Society of Sciences and Letters]]. Wróblewski published in [[jurisprudence]] and in the organization of [[Administration (government)|state administration]].
