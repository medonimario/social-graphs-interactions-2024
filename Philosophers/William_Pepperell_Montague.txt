'''William Pepperell Montague''' (11 November 1873 – 1 August 1953) was an [[Americans|American]] [[philosopher]] of the [[New realism (philosophy)|New Realist]] school. Montague stressed the difference between his philosophical peers as adherents of either "objective" and "[[critical realism (philosophy of perception)|critical realism]]".

Montague was born in [[Chelsea, Massachusetts]]. He earned his bachelors, masters, and doctorate from [[Harvard University]].<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.pdcnet.org/pdc/bvdb.nsf/purchase?openform&fp=apapa&id=apapa_2013_0143_0146_705|title=Biography: William Pepperell Montague|first=Richard T.|last=Hull|date=1 January 2013|journal=The American Philosophical Association Centennial Series|pages=143–146|accessdate=7 April 2023|via=www.pdcnet.org|doi=10.5840/apapa2013705}}</ref>  He was professor of philosophy at [[UC Berkeley]] between 1899 and 1903, and at [[Columbia University]] from 1903 to 1947. He was president of the [[American Philosophical Association]]'s eastern division in the years 1923–1924.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pragmatism.org/dmap/names_generations.htm |title=Entries in the Dictionary of Modern American Philosophers |accessdate=2008-10-13 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080820203836/http://www.pragmatism.org/dmap/names_generations.htm |archivedate=August 20, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pragmatism.org/dmap/apa_presidents.htm |title=Chronological list of Presidents of The American Philosophical Association, 1901–2000 |accessdate=2008-10-13 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080820203719/http://www.pragmatism.org/dmap/apa_presidents.htm |archivedate=August 20, 2008 }}</ref> He died in [[New York City]].

Montague was an advocate of [[panpsychism]] and proposed his own variant known as hylopsychism which developed the connection between energy and mind, contending that the physical manifestation of mind occurs as potential energy.<ref>Skrbina, David. (2017). ''Panpsychism in the West''. MIT Press. pp. 195-196</ref>
