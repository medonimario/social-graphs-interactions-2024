'''Karl Philipp Moritz''' ([[Hameln]], 15 September 1756 – [[Berlin]], 26 June 1793) was a [[German people|German]] author, editor and essayist of the ''[[Sturm und Drang]]'', late [[Age of Enlightenment|Enlightenment]], and classicist periods, influencing early [[German Romanticism]] as well. He led a life as a hatter's apprentice, teacher, journalist, literary critic, professor of art and linguistics, and member of both of Berlin's academies.

==Biography==
Moritz was born into impoverished circumstances in [[Hameln]] in 1756.<ref name=nie>{{Cite NIE|wstitle=Moritz, Karl Philipp|year=1905}}</ref> After receiving a scanty schooling, he was apprenticed to a [[hat maker]].<ref name=eb>{{EB1911|wstitle=Moritz, Karl Philipp}}</ref> After distressful attempts to gain a living, he caught the attention of a patron in [[Hanover]] and entered a [[Gymnasium (school)|gymnasium]]; however, he soon accepted an engagement as actor under [[Konrad Ekhof|Ekhof]] at [[Gotha]], failing in which he returned to study (1776) at [[Erfurt]]; but tiring again he joined the ''Herrnhuter'' ([[Moravian Church]]) at [[Barby, Germany|Barby]], and studied theology at [[Wittenberg]] (1777); then taught philanthropy at the [[Potsdam]] military orphanage, soon again to take to wandering.<ref name=amer>{{Americana|wstitle=Moritz, Karl Philipp|inline=1}}</ref>

Teaching in Berlin, he made a reputation as writer, preacher and poet, and [[Journeys of a German in England in 1782|went to England]]. Then he became professor at the gymnasium (high school) at [[Berlin]] ({{langx|de|link=no|Köllnisches Gymnasium}}). Next he tried editing the ''Vossische Zeitung'' to make it [[proletarian]], but failed. Later he traveled to Italy (1786) where he met [[Goethe]], and on his return to Germany he took up residence as Goethe's guest at [[Weimar]]. [[Karl August, Grand Duke of Saxe-Weimar-Eisenach|Duke Karl August]] helped him join the [[Prussian Academy of Sciences|Berlin Academy of Sciences]], and in 1789 Moritz became a professor of antiquities at the [[Akademie der Künste|Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Berlin]].<ref name=amer/> Among his students were [[Ludwig Tieck]], [[Wilhelm Heinrich Wackenroder]] and [[Alexander von Humboldt]]. He was an avid admirer of [[Jean Paul]], and befriended [[Moses Mendelssohn]], and [[Asmus Jakob Carstens]].

==Works==
Apart from a four-part autobiographical novel, ''[[Anton Reiser]]'', and two fictional ''Andreas Hartknopf'' novels, he also wrote a number of theoretical writings on aesthetics, especially "Über die bildende Nachahmung des Schönen" (On the Formative Imitation of Beauty), which Goethe excerpted in his ''[[Italian Journey]]''. Moritz's ''Magazin zur Erfahrungsseelenkunde als ein Lesebuch für Gelehrte und Ungelehrte'' (Journal of Experiential Psychology, as reading for scholars and laymen) was one of the first Germanophone journals of psychology.
