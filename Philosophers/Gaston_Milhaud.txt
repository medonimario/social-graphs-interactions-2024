'''Gaston Milhaud''' (10 August 1858, [[Nîmes]] – 1 October 1918, Paris) was a [[French people|French]] [[philosopher]] and [[history of science|historian of science]].

Gaston Milhaud studied mathematics with [[Gaston Darboux]] at the [[École Normale Supérieure]]. In 1881 he took a teaching post at the [[University of Le Havre]]. In 1891 he became professor of mathematics at [[Montpellier University]], and in 1895 became professor of philosophy there.<ref>J. Peiffer, 'Milhaud, Gaston', in Joseph Warren Dauben and Christoph J. Scriba, eds., ''Writing the history of mathematics: its historical development'', Birkhäuser, 2002, p.486</ref> In 1909 a chair in the history of philosophy in its relationship to the sciences was created for him at the [[University of Paris|Sorbonne]]. Milhaud's successor in the chair was [[Abel Rey]].
