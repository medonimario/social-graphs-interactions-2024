'''Ruy Mauro Marini''' (1932–1997) was a [[Brazilian people|Brazilian]] [[economist]] and [[sociologist]]. Marini is internationally known as one of the creators of [[dependency theory]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blogdaboitempo.com.br/2017/07/13/a-teoria-da-dependencia-20-anos-depois-de-ruy-mauro-marini/|title=A teoria da dependência, 20 anos depois de Ruy Mauro Marini|date=2017-07-13|website=Blog da Boitempo|access-date=2017-08-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=TnLMAgAAQBAJ&q=+marini&pg=PT191|title=Economic Development and Global Crisis: The Latin American Economy in Historical Perspective|last1=Cardoso|first1=José Luís|last2=Marcuzzo|first2=Maria Cristina|last3=Sotelo|first3=María Eugenia Romero|date=2014-02-05|publisher=Routledge|isbn=9781136735677|language=en}}</ref><ref>*[[Jaime Osorio|Osorio, Jaime]] and [[Cristobal Reyes|Reyes, Cristobal]] (2024) ''Labour Super-Exploitation, Unequal Exchange and Capital Reproduction: Writings on Marxist Dependency Theory'' ibidem Verlag, Hannover, Stuttgart.</ref> [[Super-exploitation]], and [[Unequal Exchange]]. He is the author of the work "Dialéctica de la Dependencia" (Dialectic of Dependency),<ref>[[Ruy Mauro Marini|Marini, Ruy Mauro]] (2022) ''The Dialectics of Dependency'' Trans. and Introduction [[Amanda Latimer|Latimer, Amanda]] Monthly Review Press, New York.
</ref> in which, using elements of the theory of economic development of [[Karl Marx]] adapted to the study of Latin American reality, he explains the necessity of overcoming the [[developmentalism]] of [[ECLAC]]. He was an activist of the [[Revolutionary Left Movement (Chile)|Revolutionary Left Movement]] of Chile, becoming a member of the Central Community in 1972 and director of his theoretical journal Marxismo y Revolución.

== Personal life ==
Born in [[Barbacena]], [[Minas Gerais]], he arrived in the city of [[Rio de Janeiro]] to study preparatory courses for medicine; however, he preferred personal independence, so he chose to dedicate himself to public service. In 1953, he began studying law at the Faculty of National Jurisprudence, but he did not manage to complete his student at the [[Federal University of Rio de Janeiro]]. He began studying Public Administration at the Brazilian School of Public and Business Administration and graduated in 1957.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.interpretesdobrasil.org/sitePage/78.av|title=Ruy Mauro Marini - Intérpretes do Brasil|website=www.interpretesdobrasil.org|language=pt-BR|access-date=2017-09-01}}</ref> During this period, he participated intensely in the student movement, becoming editor of the student newspaper. He founded [[Política Operária]] (POLOP) with [[Moniz Bandeira]] and [[:pt:Theotônio dos Santos|Theotônio dos Santos]], a left-wing political organization against the guidelines of the [[Brazilian Communist Party]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VBdEBAAAQBAJ&q=Pol%C3%ADtica+Oper%C3%A1ria+marini&pg=PA153|title=Intellectuals and the Search for National Identity in Twentieth-Century Brazil|last=Chilcote|first=Ronald H.|date=2014-09-08|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=9781316061886|language=en}}</ref>

He died from cancer in 1997 in Rio de Janeiro at the age of 65.
