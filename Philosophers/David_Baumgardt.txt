{{Short description|German professor of philosophy (1890–1963)}}
'''David Baumgardt''' (20 April 1890 – 21 July 1963) was an early 20th-century German Jewish [[philosopher]] in the field of philosophical history. He was a professor of philosophy at the [[University of Berlin]].<ref name="SkolnikBerenbaum2007">{{cite book|author1=Fred Skolnik|author2=Michael Berenbaum|title=Encyclopaedia Judaica, Volume 1|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=MbNYAwAAQBAJ&pg=PA54|year=2007|publisher=Granite Hill Publishers|isbn=978-0-02-865929-9|pages=54–}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Baumgardt was born in [[Erfurt]], German Empire. As a young man he studied at the universities of [[Freiburg im Breisgau|Freiburg]], [[Vienna]], [[Munich]], [[Heidelberg]] and Berlin, and served in the military during World War I.<ref name="ScholemDavid2007">{{cite book|author1=Gershom Scholem|author2=Anthony David|title=Lamentations of Youth: The Diaries of Gershom Scholem, 1913-1919|url=https://archive.org/details/lamentationsofyo0000scho|url-access=registration|year=2007|publisher=Harvard University Press|isbn=978-0-674-02669-8|pages=[https://archive.org/details/lamentationsofyo0000scho/page/355 355]–}}</ref>

==Career==
Baumgardt's main field of studies was ancient philosophy and ethics. He wrote about the relationship between ethical and religious ideology.<ref name="Iannone2013">{{cite book|author=A. Pablo Iannone|title=Dictionary of World Philosophy|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AyB6GXM-CZkC&pg=PT614|date=15 April 2013|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-134-68043-6|pages=614–}}</ref>

He emigrated to Great Britain in 1935 and to the United States in 1939.

Some years before his death he wrote a retrospective of his years at the University of Berlin, entitled, ''Looking Back on a German University Career'', which was published in 1965.<ref name="Feuer1974">{{cite book|author=Lewis Samuel Feuer|title=Einstein and the Generations of Science|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_6zauDvo78sC&pg=PA82|year=1974|publisher=Transaction Publishers|isbn=978-1-4128-2235-0|pages=82–}}</ref>

Later in his life he was a consultant in philosophy to the American [[Library of Congress]].<ref name="Reisch2005">{{cite book|author=George A. Reisch|title=How the Cold War Transformed Philosophy of Science: To the Icy Slopes of Logic|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uhahmUJndWMC&pg=PA162|date=21 March 2005|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=978-0-521-54689-8|pages=162–}}</ref>

Many commentaries were written about his work, including ''David Baumgardt and ethical hedonism'' by Zeev Levy and also ''Aristotle Ethical views'' by ''Mehdi Shokri''.

==Legacy==
Baumgardt died in [[Long Beach, New York|Long Beach]], [[New York (state)|New York]], United States.

A fellowship is offered by the Leo Baeck Institute, which houses a collection of his papers, to students who study and extend Baumgardt's work.<ref name="LLC2008">{{cite book|author=Schoolhouse Partners LLC|title=Directory of Research Grants 2008|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Z7e99f1wKjIC&pg=PA17|date=May 2008|publisher=AuthorHouse|isbn=978-1-4343-4698-8|pages=17–}}</ref>

== Main works ==

*''Franz v. Baader und die philosophische Romantik'', 1927. 
*''Der Kampf um den Lebenssinn unter Vorläufern der modernen Ethik'', 1933.
*''Bentham and the Ethics of Today'', 1952. 
*''Great Western Mystics and their Lasting Significance'', 1961. 
*''Die abendländische Mystik'', 1963.
