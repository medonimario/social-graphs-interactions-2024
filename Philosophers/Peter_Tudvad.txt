'''Peter Tudvad''' (born 27 April 1966 in [[Holme, Aarhus|Holme]] south of [[Århus]]) is a Danish [[Søren Kierkegaard]] scholar, author, philosopher and social critic, formerly at the [[Søren Kierkegaard Research Center]] and at the [[University of Copenhagen]]; he left the Søren Kierkegaard Research Center after a heated debate with colleague [[Joakim Garff]], whose Kierkegaard biography he lambasted in his own book ''Kierkegaards København''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nytfradanmark.dk/visartikel.asp?artnr=402&Category=10&kat=Boganmeldelser|title=Peter Tudvad: Kierkegaards København|language=Danish|accessdate=2010-01-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070610071202/http://www.nytfradanmark.dk/visartikel.asp?artnr=402&Category=10&kat=Boganmeldelser|archive-date=2007-06-10|url-status=dead}}</ref><!-- the actual leaving is described in the Danish Wikipedia article; the row is in the reference. -->

His 2009 book ''Nurse in the Third Reich'', an account of a Danish woman serving as a nurse in the German Red Cross, achieved some note.<ref>{{cite journal|url=http://www.danishliterarymagazine.dk/index.php?id=4127|title=Nurse in the Third Reich|journal=Danish Literary Magazine|date=Autumn 2009|accessdate=2010-01-25|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719121740/http://www.danishliterarymagazine.dk/index.php?id=4127|archivedate=2011-07-19}}</ref>
