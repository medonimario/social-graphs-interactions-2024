'''James Patrick Griffin''' (8 July 1933 &ndash; 21 November 2019)<ref name=Congress>{{cite web |title= Griffin, James, 1933- |url= http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n86124955.html |publisher= Library of Congress |access-date= 23 July 2014 |quote= Phone call to pub., 10/15/86 (James Patrick Griffin; b. 7/8/33)}}</ref> was an American-born [[philosopher]], who was [[White's Professor of Moral Philosophy]] at the [[University of Oxford]] from 1996 to 2019.

== Education ==
Griffin was educated at [[Choate Rosemary Hall|Choate School]] in [[Wallingford, Connecticut]], and [[Yale University]], obtaining a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in 1955.  He was then a [[Rhodes Scholar]] at [[Corpus Christi College, Oxford]] (1955–58), then a senior scholar at [[St Antony's College, Oxford]] (1958–60), obtaining his doctorate under the supervision of [[Gilbert Ryle]] in 1960.<ref>{{Cite journal|title=In memoriam|url=http://media.philosophy.ox.ac.uk/magazine/Oxford%20Philosophy%2011.pdf|journal=Oxford Philosophy|volume=11}}</ref><ref name=Who>{{cite web|title=Griffin, Prof. James Patrick|work=Who's Who 2009|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|date=December 2008|url=http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/article/oupww/whoswho/U18201|access-date=6 October 2009}}</ref>

== Career ==
After lecturing at [[Christ Church, Oxford]] from 1960 to 1966, he was appointed a [[Oxbridge Fellow|Fellow]] of [[Keble College, Oxford]] in 1966, a position he held until 1996. He was then appointed [[White's Professor of Moral Philosophy]] at Oxford, becoming a Fellow of Corpus Christi College; he was appointed an Honorary Fellow of Keble in 1996, and also became an Emeritus Fellow of Corpus Christi.<ref name=Who/>

In 2000 Griffin became distinguished visiting professor of philosophy at [[Rutgers University]] and in 2002 adjunct professor at the [[Centre for Applied Philosophy and Public Ethics]] in [[Australia]] in [[Canberra]].<ref name=Who/> 

Griffin visited the [[Chinese University of Hong Kong]] in spring, 2010, as its thirteenth [[Tang Chun-I Visiting Professor]],<ref>[http://www.phil.arts.cuhk.edu.hk/web/news_and_events/tcivp-intro/  Tang Chun-I Visiting Professorship,  The Chinese University of Hong Kong]</ref> conducting a four-week graduate seminar ''What, if anything, can Philosophy Contribute to Normative Ethics?'' and also spoke on ''Human Dignity as the Ground of Human Rights,'' as well as addressing the issue, ''Does 'ought' imply 'can'?'' in a public lecture.<ref>[http://www.phil.arts.cuhk.edu.hk/web/tcivp/james-griffin/  "Does ‘ough’ imply ‘can’?" 1 March 2010]</ref>

Griffin was awarded the Commission of National Education Medal from [[Poland]] (1992), the Order of Diego de Losada from [[Venezuela]] (1999) and Doctor ''honoris causa'' conferred by the [[University of Santiago de Compostela]] (2003).<ref name="CUHK profile">{{cite web | url=http://www.phil.arts.cuhk.edu.hk/web/tcivp/james-griffin/ | title=James Griffin | publisher=Philosophy Department, Chinese University of Hong Kong | date=2010}}</ref>

Griffin published five books:  ''Wittgenstein’s Logical Atomism'' (1965);<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WR7XAAAAMAAJ |title = Wittgenstein's Logical Atomism|last1 = Griffin|first1 = James|year = 1965| publisher=Clarendon Press | isbn=978-0-295-78575-2 }}</ref> ''Well-Being: Its Meaning, Measurement, and Moral Importance'' (1988);<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RJjAVOOZz_QC |title = Well-being: Its Meaning, Measurement, and Moral Importance|isbn = 9780198248439|last1 = Griffin|first1 = James|year = 1988| publisher=Clarendon Press }}</ref> ''Value Judgement: Improving our Ethical Beliefs'' (1996);<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-v3WAAAAMAAJ |title = Value Judgement: Improving Our Ethical Beliefs|isbn = 9780198235538|last1 = Griffin|first1 = James|year = 1996| publisher=Clarendon Press }}</ref> ''On Human Rights'' (2008);<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iFkalFghOa4C |title = On Human Rights|isbn = 9780191623417|last1 = Griffin|first1 = James|date = 27 August 2009| publisher=OUP Oxford }}</ref> ''What Can Philosophy Contribute to Ethics?'' (2015).<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0HHNCgAAQBAJ |title = What Can Philosophy Contribute to Ethics?|isbn = 9780191065446|last1 = Griffin|first1 = James|date = 29 October 2015| publisher=Oxford University Press }}</ref>

He died on 21 November 2019 at the age of 86.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.ccc.ox.ac.uk/News/a/Jim-Griffin-1933-2019-a-tribute/ |title=Corpus Christi College Oxford - News & Events |access-date=9 July 2020 |archive-date=31 December 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191231121439/https://www.ccc.ox.ac.uk/News/a/Jim-Griffin-1933-2019-a-tribute/ |url-status=bot: unknown }}</ref>
