'''Susan Lesley "Sue" Mendus''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|commas=on|CBE|FBA|size=100%}}, [[Fellow of the Learned Society of Wales|FLSW]] (born 25 August 1951) is a [[Welsh people|Welsh]] academic specialising in [[political philosophy]]. She is currently [[Professor Emerita]] of Political Philosophy at the [[University of York]]. She was Vice-President (Social Sciences) of the [[British Academy]] between 2008 and 2012.

==Early life and education==
Susan Coker was born on 25 August 1951 in [[Swansea]], [[Wales]], to John and Beryl Coker.<ref name="Who's Who 2016">{{Cite web |title=MENDUS, Prof. Susan Lesley |url=http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/article/oupww/whoswho/U10000493 |website=Who's Who 2016 |publisher=Oxford University Press |accessdate=30 October 2016 |date=November 2015}}</ref><ref name="Swansea Metropolitan University - Honorary Fellowship">{{Cite web |title=Welsh Political Philosopher Honoured by Swansea Met |url=http://www.smu.ac.uk/index.php/the-university/news/1540-welsh-political-philosopher-honoured-by-swansea-met |publisher=Swansea Metropolitan University |accessdate=19 February 2013 |date=23 July 2012}}</ref> She grew up in [[Waun Wen]], Swansea,<ref name="South Wales Evening Post – CBE">{{Cite news |last=Thomas |first=Geraint |title=Political philosopher gets CBE honour to think over |url=http://www.thisissouthwales.co.uk/Political-philosopher-gets-CBE-honour-think/story-17731250-detail/story.html#axzz2LMFzQ1Y1 |accessdate=19 February 2013 |newspaper=South Wales Evening Post |date=1 January 2013}}</ref> and was educated at Waun Wen Primary and Mynydd Bach schools.<ref name="Swansea Metropolitan University - Honorary Fellowship"/>

She studied classics and philosophy at [[Aberystwyth University]], graduating in 1973 with [[First Class Honours]].<ref name="South Wales Evening Post – CBE"/> After Aberystwyth she turned to [[Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford]] for a BPhil in 1975.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.levesoninquiry.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Witness-Statement-of-Professor-Susan-Mendus.pdf | title=Witness Statement from Professor Susan Mendus | work=Leveson Inquiry into the culture, practices and ethics of the press | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130323043233/http://www.levesoninquiry.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Witness-Statement-of-Professor-Susan-Mendus.pdf | archive-date=2013-03-23 | url-status=unfit }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.lmh.ox.ac.uk/alumni/prominent-alumni |title=Prominent alumni |author=<!--Not stated--> |date= |website=Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford |access-date=30 November 2020}}</ref>

In 1977, Susan Coker married Andrew Mendus and assumed her married name.<ref name="Who's Who 2016"/><ref name="South Wales Evening Post – CBE"/>

==Academic career==
In 1975, Mendus was appointed a lecturer in philosophy at the [[University of York]]. In 1986, she transferred to the Politics Department<ref name=Biography>{{Cite web |title=Susan Mendus - Biography |url=http://cds.haifa.ac.il/documents/pastconferences/isaiahberlin/berlin_mendus_cv.pdf }}</ref> and was awarded the title Professor of Political Philosophy in 1995. Between 1995 and 2000, she was Director of the Morrell Studies in Toleration Programme at the University of York.<ref name="British Academy Fellows – entry">{{Cite web |title=British Academy Fellows |url=http://www.britac.ac.uk/fellowship/elections/index.cfm?member=4488 |publisher=The British Academy |access-date=19 February 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304204318/http://www.britac.ac.uk/fellowship/elections/index.cfm?member=4488 |archive-date=4 March 2016 |df=dmy-all}}</ref>

She is a member of editorial boards of the ''British Journal of Political Science'', the ''[[Journal of Philosophy of Education]]'', and the ''[[Journal of Applied Philosophy]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Memberships |url=http://www.york.ac.uk/politics/our-staff/sue-mendus/#external |work=Professor Sue Mendus |publisher=University of York |accessdate=19 February 2013}}</ref>

Susan Mendus was called as an expert witness to the [[Leveson Inquiry]] of 2011–2012 into the culture, practices and ethics of the British press.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Research |url=http://www.york.ac.uk/politics/our-staff/sue-mendus/#research |work=Professor Sue Mendus |publisher=University of York |accessdate=19 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Professor Sue Mendus |url=http://www.levesoninquiry.org.uk/evidence/?witness=professor-sue-mendus |work=Evidence |publisher=The Leveson Enquiry |accessdate=19 February 2013 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130513090325/http://www.levesoninquiry.org.uk/evidence/?witness=professor-sue-mendus | archive-date=2013-05-13 | url-status=unfit }}</ref>

==Honours==
Susan Mendus was elected a [[Fellow of the British Academy]] (FBA) in 2004.<ref name="British Academy Fellows – entry" /> She is a Founding Fellow of the [[Learned Society of Wales]] (FLSW), which was launched in 2010.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Founding Fellows |url=http://learnedsocietywales.ac.uk/node/13 |publisher=The Learned Society of Wales |accessdate=19 February 2013}}</ref> She was awarded an [[Honorary title (academic)|Honorary Fellowship]] of [[Swansea Metropolitan University]] in July 2012.<ref name="Swansea Metropolitan University - Honorary Fellowship" /> In the 2013 [[New Year Honours]], she was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) "For services to Political Science".<ref name="LG 29 December 2012">{{London Gazette |issue=60367 |date=29 December 2012 |pages=7–8 |supp=y}}</ref>
