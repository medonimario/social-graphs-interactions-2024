'''Sir James Black Baillie''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|OBE}} (24 October 1872 &ndash; 9 June 1940) was a British [[moral philosopher]] and Vice-Chancellor of the [[University of Leeds]].  He wrote the first significant translation of [[Hegel]]'s "[[The Phenomenology of Spirit|Phenomenology of Mind]]". He is said to be the model for the character Sir John Evans in the novel ''[[The Weight of the Evidence]]'' (1944) by [[Michael Innes]].<ref name=ODNB>[[Oxford Dictionary of National Biography]] accessed 25 July 2009</ref>

==Early life and education==
Baillie was born in West Mill, Cortachy, [[Forfarshire]].  He studied  at the [[University of Edinburgh]], where he gained a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in 1899 on ''The growth of Hegel's logic'',<ref>{{Cite thesis |last=Baillie |first=James Black |date=1899 |title=Growth of Hegel's logic |language=en |publisher=University of Edinburgh|hdl=1842/23692 }}</ref> and [[Trinity College, Cambridge]].<ref>{{acad|id=BLY897JB|name=Baillie, James Black}}</ref>  

==Academic career==
Baillie lectured in philosophy at [[University of Dundee|University College, Dundee]] and in August 1902 was appointed [[Regius Professor of Moral Philosophy]] at the [[University of Aberdeen]].<ref>{{Cite newspaper The Times |title=Court Circular |date=1 August 1902 |page=7 |issue=36835}}</ref><ref>{{London Gazette|issue=27469 |page=5604 |date=29 August 1902}}</ref> 

During the [[First World War]] he was in the intelligence division of the [[British Admiralty]].  

After public service posts, Baillie was Vice-Chancellor of the University of Leeds from 1924 to his retirement in 1938.

==Honours==
Baillie was appointed an [[Order of the British Empire|Officer of the Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the [[1919 New Year Honours]],<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=31114 |supp=y |page=453|date= 8 January 1919}}</ref> made a Knight Commander of the [[Order of the Crown of Italy]] following a meeting with Italian leader [[Benito Mussolini]]<ref name=ODNB/> and received a knighthood in 1931.<ref name=LonGaz>[http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/33722/supplements/3623 London Gazette] 2 June 1931</ref>

==Marriage==
In 1906 Baillie married Helena May James.  They had no children.<ref name=ODNB/>

==Death==
Baillie died of [[prostate cancer]] in [[Weybridge]], [[Surrey]] in 1940 at the age of 67.<ref name=ODNB/>{{Better source needed|reason=The current source is insufficiently reliable ([[WP:NOTRS]]).|date=June 2024}}
