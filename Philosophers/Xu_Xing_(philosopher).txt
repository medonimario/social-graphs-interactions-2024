'''Xu Xing''' ({{zh|w=Hsü Hsing|t=許行}}; c. 372 – c. 289 BC) was a [[Chinese philosophy|Chinese philosopher]] and one of the most notable advocates of the egalitarian political philosophy of [[agriculturalism]].{{citation needed|date=April 2022}} With a group of followers he settled in the state of [[Teng (state)|Teng]] in about 315 BC. A disciple of his visited the Confucian philosopher [[Mencius]], and a short report of their conversation discussing Xu Xing's philosophy survives.<ref>{{cite journal |title=The "Nung-chia" 農 家 'School of the Tillers' and the Origins of Peasant Utopianism in China|author=A. C. Graham|journal=Bulletin of the School of Oriental and African Studies|volume=42|number=1|year=1979|pages=66–100|jstor=614828|doi=10.1017/s0041977x00108468|s2cid=154793313 }}</ref>
