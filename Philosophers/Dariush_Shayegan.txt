'''Dariush Shayegan''' ({{langx|fa|داریوش شایگان}};&lrm; 24 January 1935 – 22 March 2018) was one of the most consequential thinkers of contemporary Iran and the Near East.

== Life and career==
He was born in Tabriz from a Shia [[Iranian Azerbaijanis|Iranian Azeri]] father and a [[Georgians|Georgian]] Sunni mother; his mother descended from an aristocratic family from [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]].<ref>{{EI3|last=Jahanbegloo|first=Ramin|title=Shayegan, Dariush|year=2021|url=https://referenceworks.brillonline.com/entries/encyclopaedia-of-islam-3/shayegan-dariush-COM_40491|quote=Dariush Shayegan (Dāryūsh Shāyigān, 1935–2018), an eminent Iranian comparative philosopher and cultural critic, was born in Tabriz on 2 February 1935 to an Iranian Shīʿī Azeri merchant father and a Georgian Sunnī mother, who traced her ancestry back to an aristocratic family in Georgia.}}</ref> Shayegan studied at the [[Sorbonne University]] in [[Paris]]. He was a Professor of [[Sanskrit]] and [[India]]n religions at the [[Tehran University]].<ref>{{cite journal |last=Tavakoli-Targhi |first=Mohamad |author-link=Mohamad Tavakoli-Targhi |title=MEHRZAD BOROUJERDI, Iranian Intellectuals and the West: The Tormented Triumph of Nativism (Syracuse, N.Y.: Syracuse University Press, 1996). Pp 256. - |url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-journal-of-middle-east-studies/article/mehrzad-boroujerdi-iranian-intellectuals-and-the-west-the-tormented-triumph-of-nativism-syracuse-ny-syracuse-university-press-1996-pp-256/ED7DA92867130561C84F5CE1156CEC93 |journal=International Journal of Middle East Studies |year=2000 |volume=32 |issue=4 |pages=565–571 |doi=10.1017/S0020743800002853 |s2cid=163126695 |via=Cambridge Core}}</ref> Besides [[Persian language|Persian]], Shayegan wrote in [[French language|French]] and [[English language|English]], and spoke fluently Georgian, Russian, and Turkish (both Ottoman and Azeri).{{citation needed|date=March 2018}}. Having spent his teens at boarding school in Great Britain, Shayegan subsequently lived, during his formative years, in Geneva, where he read at the Université de Genève French literature, philosophy, Sanskrit, and political science.<ref>داریوش بوربور از هفتاد سال خاطراتش با داریوش شایگان می‌گوید؛ ژنو، شهری که شایگان در آن دوباره متولد شد»، خبرگزاری ایلنا، 2/13/ 2018 http://www.ilna.ir/بخش-فرهنگ-هنر-6/592847-ژنو-شهری-که-شایگان-در-آن-دوباره-متولد-.</ref> Shayegan received his doctorate (''doctorat de troisième cycle'') at the Sorbonne under the tutelage of his Doktorvater Henry Corbin, with a thesis entitled: ''Les relations de l'hindouisme et du soufisme d'après le "Majma’ al-Baḥrayn" de Dārā Shokūh.''

Shayegan has written many pioneering works on the epistemological specificities of eastern and western cultures and the possibility of dialogue between them. He was the founding director of the Iranian Center for the Studies of Civilizations, which launched its work in 1977 with an international symposium on the "dialogue between civilizations," a concept that has been selectively appropriated by the former Iranian President [[Mohammad Khatami]]. In 2009 Shayegan was awarded the inaugural [[Global Dialogue Prize]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.globaldialogueprize.org|title=www.globaldialogueprize.org|website=www.globaldialogueprize.org}}</ref> an international award for "outstanding achievements in the advancement and application of intercultural value research", in recognition of his dialogical conception of cultural subjectivity (for a statement of the award committee and a scholarly presentation (including a fairly complete bibliography) of Shayegan's contribution to intercultural dialogue, see the (copyrighted) webpages of this award.)

== Personal life ==
His son, [[M. Rahim Shayegan]], is an American historian and expert in history of ancient Iran.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.asriran.com/fa/news/331396 |title=پسر شایگان رئیس کرسی ایران‌شناسی دانشگاه کالیفرنیا شد |date=April 24, 2014 |access-date=April 10, 2024 |website=Asriran |archive-url=https://webcitation.org/6P7oXhdqM?url=http://www.asriran.com/fa/news/331396 |archive-date=April 26, 2014 |url-status=dead |language=fa |trans-title=Son of Dariush Shayegan was appointed to the position of Head of the Department of Iranian Studies}}</ref>

== Death ==
Shayegan died on 22 March 2018, at the age of 83 in Tehran.<ref name="mehrnews4256732">{{Cite web |url=https://www.mehrnews.com/news/4256732/داریوش-شایگان-درگذشت |title=داریوش شایگان درگذشت |date=March 22, 2018 |website=[[Mehr News Agency]] |language=fa |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180322062555/https://www.mehrnews.com/news/4256732/%D8%AF%D8%A7%D8%B1%DB%8C%D9%88%D8%B4-%D8%B4%D8%A7%DB%8C%DA%AF%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%AF%D8%B1%DA%AF%D8%B0%D8%B4%D8%AA |archive-date=March 22, 2018 |access-date=March 22, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref>

* Le regard mutilé, Schizophrénie culturelle : pays traditionnels face à la modernité, Albin English Translation : Cultural Schizophrenia, Islamic Societies Confronting the West, Translated from the French by John Howe, Saqi books, London 1992. Also published by Syracuse University Press, 1997.

* Au-delà du miroir, Diversité culturelle et unité des valeurs, Editions de l'Aube, 2004
