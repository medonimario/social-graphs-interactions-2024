'''Lorraine Code''' {{post-nominals|FRSC}} (born October 19, 1937) is Professor Emerita of Philosophy at [[York University]] in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], Canada and a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Canada]]. Her principal area of research is [[feminist epistemology]] and the politics of knowledge.

==Career==
She earned her [[Bachelor of Arts]] (BA) at [[Queen's University at Kingston|Queen's University]] and her PhD at the [[University of Guelph]] in 1978. After finishing her BA at Queen's in the 1950s, Code travelled to Germany on an exchange fellowship. She then spent the following years teaching in the United Kingdom before returning to Canada for graduate school.<ref name = ":1">{{cite web |title=GUELPH PHILOSOPHY AT 50: LORRAINE CODE |url=https://www.uoguelph.ca/arts/sites/uoguelph.ca.arts/files/public/Code%20talk.pdf |website=uoguelph.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019}}</ref>

In 1987, Code was appointed a Canada Research Fellow at [[York University]], and was later promoted to the title of Professor in the Department of Philosophy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Professor Lorraine Code |url=https://www.dur.ac.uk/ias/fellows/0607/code/ |website=dur.ac.uk |accessdate=April 23, 2019}}</ref> 

In 2006, she published "''Ecological Thinking: The Politics of Epistemic Location.''" <ref>{{cite web|last=Clough|first=Sharyn Clough|title=Ecological Thinking: The Politics of Epistemic Location|date=5 February 2007 |url=http://ndpr.nd.edu/news/25208-ecological-thinking-the-politics-of-epistemic-location/|publisher=University of Notre Dame|accessdate=15 January 2013}}</ref>

==Awards and honours==
In 1997, Code was awarded the Walter Gordon Fellowship for her research in feminist theory<ref>{{cite web |title=YORK UNIVERSITY TO BE HOME TO WORLD'S LARGEST SCHOOL OF WOMEN'S STUDIES |url=https://www.yorku.ca/mediar/releases_1996_2000/archive/062797.htm |website=yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=June 27, 1997}}</ref> and was named a Distinguished Research Professor.<ref>{{cite web |title=DR. LORNA R. MARSDEN TO BE INSTALLED AS YORK UNIVERSITY PRESIDENT |url=https://www.yorku.ca/mediar/releases_1996_2000/archive/101797-2.htm |website=yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=October 17, 1997 |quote=Receiving the title of Distinguished Research Professor at Monday's ceremony are Lorraine Code, from the department of philosophy, and Barry Lever, from the department of chemistry.}}</ref> Three years later, Code was awarded a [[The Killam Trusts|Killam Research Fellowship]], named after [[Dorothy J. Killam]], which allowed her to conduct full-time research.<ref>{{cite web |title=York researchers receive Killam Research Fellowships |url=https://www.yorku.ca/yul/gazette/past/archive/2000/040500/index.html |website=yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=April 5, 2000}}</ref>

In 2005, Code was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada]] for her work in epistemology.<ref>{{cite web |title=York U profs recognized by Royal Society of Canada |url=http://news.yorku.ca/2005/07/12/york-u-profs-recognized-by-royal-society-of-canada/ |website=news.yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=July 12, 2005}}</ref> She was also given an honorary doctorate from University of Guelph.<ref>{{cite web |title=Faculty honoured for academic achievements |url=http://yfile-archive.news.yorku.ca/2005/05/04/faculty-honoured-for-academic-achievements/ |website=yfile-archive.news.yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=May 4, 2005}}</ref> 

In 2009, she was awarded the Distinguished Woman Philosopher of the Year from the [[American Society for Women in Philosophy]].<ref name = "2016 RSC">{{cite web |title=Lorraine Code receives prestigious RSC award |url=http://laps.yorku.ca/2016/10/lorraine-code-receives-prestigious-rsc-award/ |website=laps.yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=October 4, 2016}}</ref>

In 2013, Code was awarded the Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee Medal.<ref name = "2016 RSC"/>

In 2016, Code was awarded the Ursula Franklin Award in Gender Studies by the Royal Society of Canada.<ref name = "2016 RSC"/> The following year, York University recognized her as a research leader in the Faculty of Liberal Arts and Professional Studies.<ref>{{cite web |title=York University's Research Leaders' Gala recognizes high-calibre, world-leading research|url=http://research.info.yorku.ca/2017/04/york-universitys-research-leaders-gala-recognizes-high-calibre-world-leading-research/|website=laps.yorku.ca |accessdate=April 23, 2019 |date=April 5, 2017}}</ref>
